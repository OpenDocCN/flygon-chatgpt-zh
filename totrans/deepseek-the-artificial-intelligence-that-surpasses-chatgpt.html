  1. What are the capabilities of DeepSeek models?

DeepSeek-V2 was later replaced by DeepSeek-Coder-V2, a more advanced model with 236 billion parameters. Designed for complex encoding requests, the model has a high context window of up to 128,000 tokens. A context window of 128,000 tokens is the maximum length of input text that the model can process simultaneously.

A larger context window allows the model to understand, summarize or analyze longer texts. This is a great advantage, for example, when working with long documents, books or complex dialogues.

A token is a unit of text. Often, this unit can be a word, a particle (like "artificial" and "intelligence"), or even a character. For example: "Artificial intelligence is awesome!" can be composed of four tokens: Artificial, "Intelligence", "Cool", "!".

The company's latest models, DeepSeek-V3 and DeepSeek-R1, have further cemented its position. A 671,000-parameter model, the DeepSeek-V3 requires significantly less resources than its counterparts, while achieving impressive results in various benchmark tests with other brands.

  1. How DeepSeek works

MoE (Mixture of Experts): a revolutionary architecture

At the heart of DeepSeek is an innovative architecture called “Mixture of Experts” (MoE). This approach involves dividing the model into multiple specialized sub-models, called “experts,” each trained to excel in a specific domain or task.

When a query is received, the system determines which experts are most relevant to address that query and activates only those sub-models, thereby optimizing the efficiency and accuracy of responses.

This architecture allows DeepSeek to handle a variety of complex tasks while maintaining efficient use of computing resources. By selectively activating relevant experts, the model reduces the computational load required, resulting in faster response times and reduced energy consumption.

Training and reduced cost: the secret of its effectiveness

One of the most remarkable aspects of DeepSeek is its efficiency in terms of training costs. While comparable models often require investments of several hundred million dollars, DeepSeek managed to train its R1 model with a budget of less than $6 million.

This feat was made possible by the use of innovative training methods and rigorous optimization of resources.

By adopting an open-source approach and relying on the collaboration of the global developer community, DeepSeek was able to reduce the costs associated with developing and training its model.

This collaborative strategy not only helped reduce expenses, but also accelerated the process of innovation and continuous improvement of the model.

Chapter 2: Differences between DeepSeek and ChatGPT

The emergence of DeepSeek, an open-source artificial intelligence model developed by a Chinese startup, has attracted considerable interest due to its performance and accessibility. Compared with OpenAI's ChatGPT model, DeepSeek has notable differences in design, operation, cost, and applications.

  1. Design and Architecture

ChatGPT, developed by OpenAI, is based on large language model architectures, such as GPT-3 and GPT-4. These models are designed to be versatile, capable of handling a wide range of tasks from text generation to machine translation.

However, this versatility can lead to trade-offs in terms of specialization and effectiveness for specific tasks.

In contrast, DeepSeek takes a modular approach using an architecture called “Mixture of Experts” (MoE). This architecture allows tasks to be divided into specialized subtasks, assigned to dedicated experts, which improves efficiency and accuracy for specific applications. This specialization makes DeepSeek particularly effective for technical or niche tasks, where targeted expertise is essential.

  1. Open-Source vs. Proprietary

A major distinction is the distribution model. ChatGPT is a proprietary service, accessible through a user interface developed by OpenAI, with paid subscription options for advanced features.

This centralized approach limits customization and adaptation of the model to specific user needs.

Conversely, DeepSeek is an open-source model, allowing users to download, modify, and integrate the model into various applications at no cost. This openness fosters collaborative innovation and allows for deep customization, tailored to individual or organizational needs.

  1. Cost and Accessibility

The cost of development and operation is another point of divergence. ChatGPT, due to its size and complexity of its infrastructure, requires considerable resources, which is reflected in the subscription fees for users.

DeepSeek, thanks to its optimized architecture and the use of less expensive hardware, has managed to significantly reduce training and operating costs. For example, the cost of training DeepSeek has been estimated at $5.6 million, much lower than the hundreds of millions of dollars required for comparable models.

  1. Performance and Specialization

In terms of performance, ChatGPT excels at both general and complex tasks, providing consistent and informative answers on a variety of topics. However, its versatility can sometimes result in less accurate answers for very specialized domains.

DeepSeek, with its modular approach, is designed to excel at technical and specialized tasks. It responds faster and with greater accuracy in specific domains, although its overall performance may be lower than ChatGPT for more general tasks.

  1. Applications and Use Cases

ChatGPT is widely used for various applications including content writing, translation, customer service, and education. Its ability to generate text in a smooth and natural way makes it suitable for a multitude of scenarios.

DeepSeek, due to its specialization, is particularly suited for applications requiring technical expertise, such as code generation, complex data analysis and scientific problem solving. Its ability to adapt and be integrated into various systems makes it a valuable tool for developers and researchers.

  1. Limitations and Challenges

Despite its advantages, DeepSeek has some limitations. Its effectiveness largely depends on the quality of the subject matter experts and how tasks are divided. Improper configuration or allocation of tasks can hamper its performance.

ChatGPT, while powerful, can sometimes generate inaccurate or biased answers, due to the nature of its training on large datasets. Additionally, its proprietary model limits transparency and customizability.

  1. Functionality

DeepSeek focuses on precise JavaScript data processing and retrieving relevant information from databases, making it a powerful analysis and research tool. ChatGPT, on the other hand, generates texts based on inputs and is optimized to provide human-like responses.

  1. Technology

DeepSeek uses contextualized algorithms and machine learning to analyze data. ChatGPT, on the other hand, is based on a language model that analyzed large amounts of text to understand and generate speech.

  1. Opportunities and challenges

Both technologies offer immense opportunities for companies looking to optimize their operations through artificial intelligence. DeepSeek can help make informed decisions based on accurate data analysis, while ChatGPT improves customer interaction and automates customer service.

However, there are also challenges. With DeepSeek, the complexity lies in data integration and analysis, while ChatGPT is challenged by the need for quality control of the generated content.

  1. Future Prospects

The emergence of DeepSeek marks a milestone in the field of artificial intelligence, with a focus on open-source accessibility, specialization and collaboration.

This approach could redefine how AI models are developed and used, fostering a greater diversity of applications and innovations.

ChatGPT, as a proprietary model, will likely continue to evolve to provide improved performance and better customization. However, the trend toward open-source and specialization, embodied by DeepSeek, may influence future development directions in the AI field.

In summary, although ChatGPT and DeepSeek share similar goals in natural language processing, their approaches differ significantly.

ChatGPT emphasizes versatility and accessibility, while DeepSeek stands out for its specialization, open-source accessibility, and increased efficiency for technical tasks. The choice between the two models will depend on the specific needs of the users, the nature of the tasks to be accomplished, and the available resources.

Chapter 4: DeepSeek Prompts

Effective use of DeepSeek relies on accurate and tailored prompts. A well-designed prompt guides the AI to generate relevant and useful responses. In this chapter, we will explore techniques for creating effective prompts, illustrated with practical examples.

  1. Understanding the role of prompts

A prompt is an instruction or question given to the AI to get a specific response. The quality of the prompt directly influences the relevance and accuracy of the response generated.

Clear and detailed prompts allow DeepSeek to understand the context and purpose of the request, leading to more satisfactory results.

  1. Techniques for writing effective prompts

To write effective prompts and get high-quality responses with DeepSeek, it is crucial to follow specific principles that allow the AI to better understand your expectations and generate relevant and tailored results. Here is a detailed explanation of each technique:

Be specific and detailed

A specific prompt helps the AI understand exactly what you want. Rather than asking a vague question like “Write an article about AI,” it’s better to specify the aspects you want the AI to cover.

For example, “Write a 500-word article on AI applications in healthcare, with a focus on medical diagnostics.” This allows the AI to focus on a particular topic, providing more relevant and detailed information. The clearer and more specific the prompt, the more targeted and in-depth the AI will be able to respond.

Define the role of AI

Assigning a role to the AI helps guide its behavior and the tone of the response. This is especially useful to ensure that the response is appropriate for the situation and the target audience.

For example, if you are asking AI to generate a marketing strategy, it is more effective to specify: “As a digital marketing expert, propose a strategy to increase the visibility of a technology startup.” This specific role encourages AI to adopt a more professional tone and strategic approach, while remaining in line with expectations.

Specify the format of the response

Clearly stating the format of the response makes it easier to organize and structure the information generated. For example, if you need a concise summary, a list of key points, or a detailed essay, mention this in your prompt.

For example: “Write a bulleted list of the pros and cons of artificial intelligence in education.” This type of detail helps the AI adapt to your preferences for presenting information.

Use examples

Providing examples can be very helpful in clarifying your expectations. If you are asking AI to generate catchy headlines for an article, provide a template or direction to follow.

Iteration is about gradually improving a prompt based on the results obtained. If the initial response is not what you expected, it does not mean that the AI is limited. Sometimes, it is enough to rephrase the prompt, add relevant information, or specify the aspects to be explored.

Using contexts

Context is crucial for AI to understand the environment or setting of the request. For example, if you ask AI to write a content strategy for a website, it is important to provide additional information such as the target audience, the company’s products or services, and current market trends.

Adding these details allows the AI to generate a more targeted response, so that it is directly applicable to your specific situation. This allows the AI to better adapt to specific constraints, offering more precise suggestions that are consistent with the context of your request.

Clarification of expectations

Specifying the response format is essential for the AI to optimally meet your needs. For example, if you want a summary, specify it. If you need a detailed analysis or a comparison in table form, clearly mention it in the prompt.

This allows the AI to structure the response appropriately, making it easier to read and apply the information. By adding hints about the desired tone or length (e.g., “write a 300-word article” or “present a bulleted list”), you direct the AI to a response that better fits your style or specific needs.

In summary, these three tips help you optimize your interaction with AI and get more relevant, tailored, and actionable answers. Iteration improves accuracy, context ensures relevance, and clarifying expectations allows you to have a structured response that meets your exact needs.

  1. Avoiding Common Mistakes

1. Ambiguity

Ambiguity in a prompt can lead to vague or unhelpful responses. It’s crucial to be specific in how you phrase your requests. For example, rather than simply asking, “Tell me about AI,” specify what you’re expecting, such as, “What are the ethical challenges associated with using AI in recruiting?” This specificity helps the AI understand exactly what you’re looking for, avoiding overly general or off-topic responses.

2. Lack of context

Lack of context often leaves AI confused about the purpose of your query. For more relevant results, you should always provide enough information about the situation or background. For example, instead of asking “What are the challenges of project management?”, you can specify “What are the challenges of project management in a growing tech startup?” This gives AI the information it needs to tailor its response to a specific setting.

3. Conflicting Instructions

Conflicting instructions can confuse the AI, resulting in inconsistent or inappropriate responses. For example, asking “Write a formal article about informal fashion trends” is a conflicting request because a formal article and informal trends don’t go well together.

So it’s critical to make sure your requests are consistent and aligned with a common goal. This ensures that AI produces responses that meet the tone and expectations you’ve set.

In summary, to avoid common mistakes, you need to be as clear and specific as possible in your requests, providing enough context and ensuring that the instructions are consistent. This allows the AI to generate relevant responses that are tailored to your needs.
