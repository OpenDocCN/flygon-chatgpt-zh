What’s a Prompt, and How Do I Write One?

A prompt is simply the instruction you give ChatGPT.

It’s what you type in to tell it what you want — like asking a colleague to “write up a quick update for the owner,” or “turn this into something we can send to the tenant.” That’s it. No special format. No magic words. Just a plain-English request.

If you’ve ever written an email asking someone else for help wording something, you’ve already written a prompt — you just didn’t call it that.

Here’s what a basic prompt might look like:

“Write a professional message to a tenant explaining that the repair is delayed. Make it sound calm and apologetic, but clear.”

Or:

“Turn these notes into a short update for the landlord:

plumber attended Wednesday

parts ordered, due Monday

tenant updated and happy to wait”

You don’t need perfect grammar or fancy instructions.
You can use bullet points, rough drafts, or even voice notes (if you’re on mobile). The more clearly you explain what you want, the better the result will be — but you don’t have to overthink it.

If you’re ever unsure where to start, try this simple prompt:

“I need to send a message about [topic]. Can you help me write it in a professional and polite tone?”

That’s enough.

You already know what you want to say — ChatGPT just helps you say it better, faster, and with less second-guessing.

Prompting Do’s and Don’ts (For Property Managers)

Once you get the hang of prompting, it starts to feel natural — but here are a few simple do’s and don’ts to help you get better results right from the start.

Do: Use dot points or rough notes
You don’t have to write a full paragraph. ChatGPT is great at turning fragments into complete, professional messages.
Example prompt:
“Turn these notes into an email to the owner: leak reported Tuesday, plumber attended Friday, roof tiles cracked, waiting on quote.”

Do: Tell it what tone you want
You can ask ChatGPT to make your message sound polite, firm, friendly, neutral, apologetic — whatever suits the situation.
Example prompt:
“Rewrite this to sound professional but not too formal.”

Do: Treat it like a draft, not a final version
Always read over what it gives you. You might want to add personal details, double-check facts, or adjust phrasing to match your agency’s style.

Don’t: Copy and paste without checking
Even good responses might miss a detail or use phrasing that’s slightly off. Always proofread before sending — especially for legal, financial, or tribunal-related content.

Don’t: Be vague
If you just write “write an email,” you won’t get something useful. Be clear about what it’s about, who it’s for, and how you want to come across. The more you give, the better the result.

Don’t: Expect it to know your policies
ChatGPT doesn’t know your agency’s internal procedures, your landlord’s preferences, or your local legislation. You still need to guide it — just like you would a new staff member.

Think of ChatGPT like a helpful assistant who’s great at writing but needs clear direction. The better you explain what you need, the more useful it becomes.

ChatGPT makes this part easier by helping you shape your tone without rewriting from scratch.

You can ask it to:

  • Make your message sound more friendly
  • Add warmth to something that feels too blunt
  • Reword a message to sound more professional
  • Adjust the level of formality to suit the recipient

For example:

Original prompt:
“I need to tell a tenant their maintenance request can’t be completed until next week.”

You could ask ChatGPT:

  • “Make this sound friendly but firm.”
  • “Add a polite and apologetic tone without sounding overly formal.”
  • “Rewrite this in a way that reassures them we’re on top of it.”

Tone is one of the biggest contributors to misunderstandings, especially over email. When you’re rushed, tired, or dealing with conflict, it’s easy for a message to come across too sharp — or, just as unhelpfully, too vague.

With ChatGPT, you can shift tone quickly, clearly, and consistently. It helps you stay calm, sound composed, and communicate with the right balance of empathy and authority — no matter what kind of day you’re having.

Clear, respectful wording you can copy, customise, or prompt ChatGPT to expand on — perfect for inspections, maintenance, and everyday updates.

Routine Inspection Reminder (SMS or Email)

Prompt:
Write a short, polite message to a tenant reminding them of their upcoming routine inspection on [Date] at [Time]. Include the property address and invite them to get in touch with any questions.

Example Output:
Hi [Tenant Name],
Just a reminder that your routine inspection is scheduled for [Day, Date] between [Time] at [Property Address]. Please let us know if you have any questions or need to make alternative arrangements.
Kind regards,
[Your Name / Agency Name]

Follow-Up After Inspection

Prompt:
Write a friendly email to a tenant thanking them for access during the inspection. Mention that the property was in good condition, with a couple of minor items noted.

Example Output:
Hi [Tenant Name],
Thank you for providing access for today’s routine inspection. The property was presented in good condition. We’ve noted a couple of small items (e.g. light mould in the bathroom, general surface cleaning in kitchen) and will follow up if needed.
Feel free to reach out if you have any questions.
Warm regards,
[Your Name]

Cleaning Request (Light Reminder)

Prompt:
Write a polite message to a tenant following an inspection, requesting attention to some minor cleaning items in the kitchen and bathroom. Keep the tone respectful and constructive.

Example Output:
Hi [Tenant Name],
Thanks again for allowing access for the recent inspection. We just wanted to flag a couple of minor cleaning areas to keep an eye on — particularly in the kitchen and bathroom. Nothing urgent, but your attention to these before the next inspection would be appreciated.
Please let us know if you have any questions.
Kind regards,
[Your Name]

Access Notice (Friendly and Compliant)

Prompt:
Write a respectful access notice for a scheduled trade visit. Include the date, time, purpose, and a note about key access if applicable.

Example Output:
Hi [Tenant Name],
We’ve scheduled a tradesperson to attend [Property Address] on [Date] between [Time] to carry out [brief description, e.g. a plumbing repair].
If you won’t be home, access will be arranged using the agency-held keys in line with your tenancy agreement. Please contact us if you’d prefer to be present or have any questions.
Best regards,
[Your Name]
