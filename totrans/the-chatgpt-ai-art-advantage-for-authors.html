Preface

As authors, we pour our hearts and souls into crafting stories that inspire, entertain, and enlighten readers. But let's be real—writing an exceptional book is only step one. To actually sell our masterpieces, we authors need to become savvy marketers and illustrators. And if you're anything like me, that can feel overwhelming, because frankly, they're entirely different skill sets.

Traditionally, many new authors dream of landing publishing deals, seduced by the promise of professionals handling all that marketing and illustration stuff. Imagine just writing, while someone else magically transforms your words into a beautifully packaged product that practically sells itself. Sounds dreamy, right? But in reality, scoring a traditional publishing deal can be harder than writing the book itself, especially for emerging authors or less-famous experts. That's why so many of us have stepped into the wild, wonderful world of self-publishing. It’s liberating, exciting—and, let’s admit it, sometimes a little terrifying.

Sure, editing and feedback often come easily from our supportive networks of family and friends (or the occasional brutally honest reviewer). But professional-quality graphics? That’s another beast entirely. Skilled illustrators are incredible, but their rates can quickly empty our coffee budgets (and we authors love our coffee).

Thankfully, the creative landscape is shifting in our favour. Enter AI technologies like ChatGPT. Suddenly, graphically-challenged authors (yes, I’m raising my hand here) can whip up stunning visuals simply by writing clear, creative prompts. True, human illustrators remain unmatched, but AI offers a practical, affordable way for indie authors to generate eye-catching visuals consistently, helping our books stand out in a crowded market.

Welcome to this exciting new era, my fellow authors, where authors—even those of us who can barely draw a stick figure—can become visual storytellers, enhancing our books and captivating readers through the magic of images.

Mastering Image Prompts

Communicating clearly with AI should be easy for us as authors, after all we are masters of word, storytelling and instruction – all we have to learn is how to wield our words to create concrete visual images. If we authors can create worlds, people, plots and passions via our books, why not do it AI?

Here’s how to twist AI to our genius.

Let’s take a look at the basic anatomy of the prompt.

Basic Prompt

Create an image of <a senior woman author> <happily> <typing on her laptop> in a <sunlit home office in the Himalayas> Include <cozy décor> and <a coffee mug> on her <desk.>

All the content within brackets can be changed – from left to right, these specify the:

  • person
  • emotion
  • action
  • setting

Note: Do not include the <> in your prompt.

Now let’s get Chat to improve or change this image.

Maybe she is a bit too senior and you want an image that will apply to a wider age group:

Prompt

Make her a bit younger and with salt and pepper hair

You'll wait a few seconds for the results to process, and then you can either accept the changes or ask for some more tweaks.

You decide that you want to use this image for a post to promote a writing retreat in the Himalayas.

So, you prompt Chat:

Include floating text - Come to the Writing Retreat in the Himalayas.

You can get the results you're looking for via a simple conversation with the image interface. You don’t have to learn coding or hire an expert!

Notice how AI kept the character and most other elements the same, rather than starting from scratch?

You can also change many different things, such as telling it to change it from a senior female to a senior male or having them work on their laptop on a wraparound porch outside rather than an indoor home office.

You can also change the emotion. You can pretty much use this prompt to get infinite different images.

As you can see from the previous example, ChatGPT 4o is much easier than some of the more complex image generating tools online. But you still must know how to prompt for what you need and want. You’re a writer – so begin with being specific and thinking from a visual standpoint as well as a strategic one for your marketing efforts.

The best way to get a prompt is to imagine there is a human graphic designer sitting in front of you and you have to tell them what you need. You would never simply tell someone to “make a powerful marketing image.” That's not enough for it to go on.

You want to specify :

  • what emotion the image should convey,
  • any camera angles you may want,
  • the lighting in the picture,
  • the background or lack of background if you want it transparent, the colour palette to be used,
  • any specific objects you want in the image,
  • the style.

No more having to manually buy stock images and then go to another tool and add text yourself. ChatGPT’s 4o can do it for you.

This was done in seconds. No need for complex tools for images that wipe away your budget and valuable writing time.

Book Covers and Product Graphics: Create Stunning Covers With or Without a Designer

The other thing that takes up your time as an author is covers, either for books, bonuses or blogs.

This is where I often struggle for ideas or time or budget.

To use ChatGPT to create an info product cover, you want to include other details such as

  • the product you are selling
  • the niche you are in, and
  • the emotional tone you're trying to convey.

You'll also specify:

  • what the title should be and
  • where it should be placed or which type of font you want.
  • the background image that should be used.

For example, you might say something like this where you tell Chat exactly what you want.

Prompt

Create a full-size e-book cover for the book titled “Easy Images for Authors Who Can’t Draw.”

  • Use a clean, modern sans-serif font for the title at the top.
  • In the centre, place a badly drawn doodle of a sun, crooked house, and stick tree.
  • Make the doodle appear like a floating sticker or hologram , with subtle drop shadow or glow.
  • Use a light blue textured background (no white).
  • At the bottom, place “Your Name” in a refined script font.
  • Ensure professional layout, spacing, and proportion (6x9 aspect ratio).

And this is the result – you could use this directly or you could use this to give to your book cover designer. That alone will save you a lot of time and money, as most designers will only do a limited number of iterations of your book cover before they have to ask you for more fees.

You can play around with it to get what you want, whether it’s a different font, layout, or graphic.

You can also ask Chat to share the prompt it created to generate the images. Yes, I know, you gave it a simple prompt, but Chat creates its own prompt and then generates the image.

So now let’s use that to add a subtitle:

Prompt

Create a full size vertical e-book cover for a book titled "Easy Images for Authors Who Can’t Draw".

Use the following design elements:

  • Title at the top in a clean, modern sans-serif font , bold and centre-aligned
  • A subtitle just below the title in smaller sans-serif font:
  • “Visual Content Creation for Writers Who’d Rather Just Write”
  • In the centre, a badly drawn doodle of a sun, crooked house, and stick tree.
  • Make the doodle appear to float slightly , with a subtle glow or shadow.
  • Use a light blue watercolour-textured background —not plain white.
  • At the bottom, place "Your Name" in an elegant script font.
  • Ensure clean layout, whitespace balance, and professional proportions.

Now you can download the image and send it to your book cover designer.

  • Or keep creating. (but don’t spend too much time!)
  • Or use it for your product graphics in your marketing directly.
  • Or generate promotional images
  • Or create an image for a related book, such as book covers of a series that you haven not yet created, but you want to place at the end of the book so that users can buy it when it is ready.

Continuity in Prompting

As you can see, it doesn’t always get it perfect on the first try. No image generator does that all the time because it’s not able to read your mind. But with a few small tweak prompts, you can get what you want.

Nobody wants to have to start from scratch, and now you don't have to. Just type your instructions like you would revisions to a human designer, whether it's a small or large adjustment using a follow-up prompt. If you want to keep most of the image but just change certain things or one element, be very direct in telling it to: “Keep everything about this image, but change [item] to [preference].”

ChatGPT now understands that you are referencing the previous image, which is something DALL-E didn't grasp. You don't have to retype your entire original prompt or redescribe anything.

You can be specific about whether or not you want to zoom in or zoom out of the picture, add more white space, correct a hand that may have too many fingers or a weird facial expression, and more.

Sometimes, you may want AI to start over from scratch. If that's the case, clean up your prompt instead of trying to patch up the original output. You can even ask AI to help you revise the prompt so that it gives you more of what you're looking for.

Prompt:

Create a digital image of a distressed woman in her 30s with shoulder-length, wavy brown hair standing in a city setting. She is holding a book titled "MY PRODUCT", with the entire title clearly visible (no cropping). Her expression should show frustration or confusion—wide eyes, furrowed brow, parted lips. Use a blurred urban background and bold red text that reads: “WHY IS NO ONE BUYING MY PRODUCT?” The book title must be fully legible and uncropped.

Keeping Your Brand’s Visual Identity Consistent

From a marketing perspective, you want consistency for your graphics across all platforms. That may mean keeping it consistent in terms of the colour palette, for example. You have to be very specific with ChatGPT 4o when doing this.

You may already know not to say something like, “make it colourful.” That gives AI the ability to pull any colour under the sun into your image, which may not be what you want. But it's still not enough to simply say, “Use greens and blues” if you have a specific colour palette that needs to be used.

If you want it to use Forest Green, tell it to use Hex# 228B22. If you want dark blue, you might tell it to use Hex# 1B2D48. These specifics give you exactly what you want without question, and ensures the graphics are consistent at all times.

In some cases, you might even have a character or some sort of cartoon image that you want consistently used. This is somewhere that AI might veer off course. Sometimes, you can tell it to use the same person in an image, and it will maintain that character's exact look.

But if it ever changes things, you can always upload the image you want replicated as part of your prompt so that it knows exactly which character you're referring to.

One thing that might help is for you to keep a library or checklist of these specific prompts and images so that you can pull them up at any time and quickly feed them into ChatGPT 4o whenever you need something maintained in a consistent manner.

Ethical Considerations

Let’s get something straight: just because AI can do something, doesn’t mean it’s smart—or ethical—for your business.

You might see people in online groups showing off how ChatGPT-4o can generate stunning images of celebrities that “don’t even look AI-generated.” But here’s the truth: using a celebrity likeness in your marketing, ads, or product visuals is a legal and ethical landmine .

Even if it’s “just an image,” if you’re implying a celebrity endorses your brand—or if their face shows up anywhere near your sales funnel—you could be held liable. You wouldn’t want someone using your name or face to sell their product without your permission. So don’t do it to someone else.

This goes double if you’re working in a regulated niche like health, finance, or education . You must ensure that your visuals you use don’t mislead your audience or trigger compliance issues. AI-generated images can be convincing , but that doesn’t mean they’re safe. That is up to your human ethics and judgement.

Logos

If the image generator pulls inspiration from existing logos—even subtly—you could end up with a design that accidentally copies a trademark. That’s not just unoriginal; it’s a lawsuit waiting to happen.

So, before you cancel your Photoshop or Midjourney subscription in favour of ChatGPT’s new native image tools, take time to test and explore responsibly . Use your ChatGPT Plus access to build a prompt library that reflects your voice, your brand, and your values.

The image tools in GPT-4o are fast, flexible, and surprisingly good—but they’re not a shortcut to skip best practices. Think of them as a creative co-pilot , not a legal free pass.

The real win? You can build and refine your visuals right inside the same space you’re already writing in. That means less waiting, fewer tools to juggle, and a faster feedback loop for all your marketing needs.
