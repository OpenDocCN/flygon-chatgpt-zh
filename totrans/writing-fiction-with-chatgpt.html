There is no doubt the world—from regulators to companies to private citizens—must figure out the rules for this new technology. While they’re doing that, let’s not throw the baby out with the bathwater.

This is likely the opposite of what you’ve heard. Not to worry—take some time with me in these pages, and I’ll show you what ChatGPT really is and why I’m excited to use it in my writing business.

Let’s start with what ChatGPT is. Have you ever seen a bubble on the bottom right of a website screen that says “Need help?” If you click on it, the first thing it does is ask you to type your question. When you do, it will either give you the answer you are looking for or, if it can’t locate an answer, it will respond that it needs more info. If your question is particularly unusual, you’ll get handed off to a (human) agent. These are called chatbots and are one of the earliest examples of Artificial Intelligence—programs designed to answer standard questions in a way that feels like an organic interaction.

ChatGPT is a really, really good chatbot.

What is it that makes it so good? In the pages to come, you’ll start to get an inkling of what it can and, perhaps more importantly, can’t do. I’ll show you how to formulate your questions so that it does understand, and if it doesn’t, I’ll show you how to figure out what to do next. Word of warning: because of the way A.I. works, there will be times when it seems as if it is making stuff up. Here are some things ChatGPT can’t do:

  • It doesn’t understand language or context.
  • It can’t work without a human giving it instructions.
  • It doesn’t know if something is true or not.
  • It can’t make decisions about copyright or plagiarism.
  • It only contains information up to April 2023 (although this may change periodically).

I am currently working with a wonderful editor, Alice Sudlow, to get my fiction book The Call to Witchery ready for publication. A weak area of the story is the internal character journey. To help me strengthen this skill, Alice asked me to map out what emotional stages a character would go through when moving from self-rejection to self-acceptance, keeping in mind we are talking about magic and a hero’s journey. During our discussion, we also touched on the five stages of grief that a character might go through as they move through the character arc. It made sense, but I wasn’t sure how everything was going to fit together.

This would be challenging for an author, even with a human assistant. Through a series of prompts, I was able to get ChatGPT to map out all of the emotional stages my character would go through when moving from self-rejection of her magic to reluctant self-acceptance within the context of the hero’s journey, while incorporating the five stages of grief and any other psychotherapy frameworks that might apply. Researching those frameworks, understanding the concepts, and meshing them together would have taken me weeks, if not months. This work took me a day, and most of that time was just asking guided questions and reading what ChatGPT answered. ChatGPT itself processed my questions in about two minutes (it was probably faster—I didn’t time it exactly). That is crazy fast. Indirectly, ChatGPT is helping me learn the craft.

Writing is still about us, our ideas, and our stories, even when our tools change.

So, here’s to exploring the future of writing, making sense of A.I., and doing what we do best: telling stories.

CHAPTER 1

UNDERSTANDING CHATGPT

Before we get to ChatGPT, we’re going to start with its precursor, Artificial Intelligence (A.I.).

If you picked up a paper anytime in the last decade, chances are there was an article about Artificial Intelligence in it. But that doesn’t mean we learned anything about it. In my mind, the whole thing had to do with robots building cars. Until ChatGPT was released, I had never made connections beyond that. As it started to get lots of attention in the writing world, my curiosity finally aroused enough to check it out. But what was “it”?

A quick Google search for a definition of A.I. yielded a common theme, encapsulated well by IBM:

“Artificial intelligence leverages computers and machines to mimic the problem-solving and decision-making capabilities of the human mind.”

Focus on the word mimic: Computers and machines cannot think like humans. We are complex creatures, and the way we think and make decisions reflects that complexity. Just ask the many doctors, neuroscientists, psychologists, psychiatrists, and medical researchers in the world. Their work has shed a light on our capabilities and limitations, yet the essence of being human continues to elude them. A.I. has leveraged all that research to design the programs that run in a way that feels more human than machine.

Something to keep in mind: The humans building this technology need to identify all possible scenarios a user would encounter. Humans are, well, human and do sometimes miss things. In computer lingo, these are called bugs. We’ve all found them when we get that endless looping circle or, worse, the computer freezes. The program doesn’t know what to do and so it keeps spinning. Part of the regular updates you are asked to install are “patches” for bugs found. A.I. is less ‘buggy’ and that’s because the kinks got worked out with the earlier chatbots.

To understand how we got here, let’s have a look at the brief history which made A.I. possible.

A Brief History of A.I.

1985—IBM begins development on True Blue, with the goal of creating a computer system capable of playing chess at a grandmaster level.

1995—Richard Wallace, MIT, creates first A.I. chatbot called A.L.I.C.E. (Artificial Linguistic Internet Computer Entity) (A quick side note: a chatbot is a computer program designed to simulate conversation with human users, especially over the internet.)

1997—True Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov in a six-game match, marking a significant milestone in the development of artificial intelligence.

2004—IBM creates Watson, also an A.I. chatbot, with the idea of having a software beat a human in the game of Jeopardy. Once this was achieved, IBM turned its attention to making it profitable. They worked with other companies to create apps using Watson technology. Today, it is used by 70 percent of the global banking institutions, has over 100 million users, and is used by thirteen out of fourteen system integrators

2011—Apple releases Siri, regarded as one of the most popular examples of artificial intelligence.

2014—Microsoft creates and launches Cortana. Originally developed for Windows Phone 8.1, it alerts users to upcoming meetings, answers user questions, searches user’s PC and web, sets reminders, shows the weather, and opens applications. (This has since been disabled.)

2016—Google announces Allo at developer conference. Allo was an instant messaging mobile app for the Android and iOS mobile operating systems. It was touted as having strong privacy features, however, this was significantly rolled back as Google tried to improve the “smart reply” feature in the app. (Amidst a number of issues, Google discontinued it in March 2019.)

2017—The first AI-based chatbot platforms for businesses are created - Apple Siri, Facebook Messenger, and Chatfuel. While we are all familiar with Siri and Messenger, Chatfuel is the chatbot platform for Facebook Messenger and is used to increase sales, reduce costs, and automate support on Facebook.

2018—Development for ChatGPT begins by OpenAI. Self-driving cars hit the road.

2022—ChatGPT released for public use.

As you can see, ChatGPT cannot be categorized as an overnight success. And yet, it took most of us by surprise. Myself included.

In the beginning, I thought it was just a souped-up Google. It gave me answers on topics, which I fact-checked with Google, in complete sentences rather than just a list of websites. I was impressed with how it formed those sentences, but that wasn’t enough for me to truly grasp how remarkable the technology is.

It wasn’t until I took a course from Jon Morrow, a highly successful blogger teaching students how to use ChatGPT for blogging. There, the capabilities of ChatGPT were revealed and the lightbulb went on for me. If ChatGPT could do that much for blogging, imagine what it could do for fiction.

Thus, my six-month journey began. This book is designed to save you that time by showing you what this tool can do for fiction authors. The first step is to understand the technology driving it.

How Does ChatGPT work?

“ChatGPT is the first chatbot platform to combine both AI-powered natural language processing (NLP) and Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) models.”

This is the sentence you will read the most often whenever you ask Google for a definition of a ChatGPT. You’ll scratch your head and ask yourself “What does this even mean?” Let’s figure it out. First, we’ll break that definition into two parts: a) AI-powered Natural Language Processing (NLP); and b) Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) model.

AI-powered Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Imagine you have a magical parrot named Polly. Now, Polly is smart enough to communicate with us. Here’s how:

  1. AI-powered: Polly’s magical abilities come from a potion (let’s call it the A.I. potion) that she sipped as a chick. This potion didn’t just give her the ability to mimic words, but to make connections and generate language based on patterns she’s heard before. When she hears thousands of people talk about apples being fruits, she learns the connection between “apple” and “fruit.” Over time, with more conversations and exposure to more language, Polly becomes smarter and more adept at understanding nuances, jokes, and even complicated questions.
  2. Natural Language: Just like humans, Polly speaks and understands human language—whether it’s asking for a cracker, keeping up with a story you tell her, or even making up a new tale.
  3. Processing: Polly isn’t just any parrot. She can process, or think deeply about, the words she hears and says. If you show her a list of groceries, she can tell you which items are fruits and which are vegetables. She listens, thinks, and responds.

Whenever you hear “AI-powered Natural Language Processing,” you can think of Polly—a magical parrot that not only understands and speaks human language but also thinks deeply about it, all thanks to a special A.I. potion. By the way, Large Language Models (LLM) is another term being used for this functionality.

But Polly is only half of the equation of ChatGPT. She can mimic our speech, but that’s not much use to us. We also need her to be able to access information so that we can ask questions and have her return an answer. This is where Luna comes in.

Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) Model

Imagine a massive library that contains everything ever written (up to April 2023). Sitting at a grand desk in the centre of the library is a special librarian named Luna. Here are Luna’s special skills:

  1. Generative: Luna doesn’t just answer with yes or no, or just point you to a book. Instead, she can create or “generate” new sentences or even stories based on what she has learned. (If you’ve seen the term “Generative A.I.”, it means the same thing.)
  2. Pre-trained: Before you ask Luna (our model) a question, understand that she has already read almost every book in the library. She hasn’t memorized them word for word, but she’s great at understanding and remembering the essence or pattern of things. This reading phase is what we call “pre-training.” In this phase, Luna was given vast amounts of text to understand language patterns, structures, and meanings.
  3. Transformer: Now, Luna has a special way of organizing and recalling information. When you ask her a question, she doesn’t just remember one book. Instead, she recalls bits and pieces from several books, figuring out the relationships between words, sentences, and contexts. This method of hopping around and connecting information from different sources is the essence of the “transformer” architecture. It can focus on different parts of a sentence to understand context, much like the way we pay attention to specific words when we listen. It’s like a super-efficient system of understanding and generating responses based on numerous references.

So, think of GPT like a well-read librarian who can creatively answer your questions by combining everything she has learned from the vast amount of content she has read.

What’s in the library?

Luna’s library is actually large collections of text data, such as books, articles, and web pages that were available on the web up to April 2023 through a nonprofit organization called Common Crawl. It gathers information from the web and freely provides its datasets for anyone to access. Luna didn’t read this data the same way a human reads a book for comprehension, enjoyment, or learning. Instead, “reading” in this context refers to Luna processing and analyzing vast amounts of text to understand patterns.

What do we mean by patterns?

Patterns occur when data repeats in a predictable way. Think of sentence structure. A simple sentence is made up of a subject + a verb + possibly an object. For example, “The lady walked to her car.” Over the course of Luna’s education, she would have been exposed to this type of sentence over and over again. She would have recognized it as a pattern, which would tell her to use it as a way to structure her responses. This exposure was repeated for all types of sentences and sequences of words (for example, the word “the” will never appear twice together, because not seeing something is also a type of pattern).

Here’s how Luna learned:

  1. Vast Collections, but Not Everything: Think of Luna’s magical library as a representation of the internet filled with many bookshelves, cabinets, and scrolls. Even with her dedication, she hasn’t gone through every single item in the library. It is simply too vast for that. She’s done her best to cover a wide range, ensuring she gets a mix of topics, styles, and perspectives.
  2. Common Crawl Shelf: One of her most extensive bookshelves is labeled “Common Crawl.” This shelf contains many volumes summarizing a broad range of web pages. Luna spends a good chunk of her time perusing these volumes, gaining insights from various corners of the web.
  3. Gaps in Knowledge: Despite her extensive reading, there are corners of the library Luna hasn’t reached. Sometimes, a visitor might ask about a niche topic from a book she hasn’t read, or a unique story she hasn’t come across. While she can make educated guesses based on what she knows, there are limits to her knowledge.

ChatGPT = Luna and Polly Together

Think of ChatGPT as having a magical parrot and a well-read librarian working together to answer your questions. It understands and generates human language based on patterns it’s seen before, and it can reference vast amounts of information to inform that language. Together, they’re able to generate new sentences or stories in “conversation” with you.

Luna is sitting at the grand desk in the center of her magical library, with Polly perched on her shoulder. You approach them with a question (or a “prompt”):

  1. Receiving the Prompt: Asking your question is like handing Luna a puzzle piece. She looks at this piece and thinks, “Where have I seen something like this before?”
  2. Searching the Library: Instead of manually searching through stacks of books, Luna has a magical table. Above this table floats snippets and summaries of every book she’s read, representing the vast knowledge and patterns she’s familiar with.
  3. Transformer Magic: Wearing her special glasses (symbolizing the “transformer” mechanism), Luna sees connections between the floating snippets. If you ask about apples, she might find links to fruits, recipes, stories about orchards, and more. These glasses allow her to focus on the most relevant snippets to see how they’re interconnected.
  4. Forming a Response: Luna pieces together the interconnected snippets to form a coherent and informative mini-story or explanation.
  5. Polly’s Touch: Before Luna voices the answer, she consults with Polly. The magical parrot, having listened to countless human conversations, tweaks the response to ensure it sounds natural, flows well, and captures any common phrases or sayings. Polly ensures that the answer doesn’t just relay information, but does so in a way that feels human.
  6. Delivering the Answer: With their combined expertise, Luna and Polly present the answer to you. It’s their best collaborative effort to address your question based on all the knowledge and linguistic patterns they’ve encountered in their magical library and conversations.

Together, Luna’s vast knowledge and ability to make intricate connections paired with Polly’s flair for making conversations feel intuitive and human, represent the heart of ChatGPT.

CHAPTER 2

USING CHATGPT FOR FICTION WRITING

If you’ve been following news stories and blogs about ChatGPT, you’ll already be aware ChatGPT-generated books are already on sale on Amazon. There are even blog posts published by some writers about the process they used and, of course, how much money they are making from the endeavor. As a technologist, I see this and think, “It’s the Wild, Wild West.”

Here we go again. At the beginning of the internet, if a company had the word “Web” in its name, chances were they would get funding from Venture Capital firms. All kinds of programs were developed (most never went anywhere). People were in a frenzy, excited about the possibilities. Doomsday champions were pounding the pavement indicating it was the end of the world. Twenty years later, the web has matured to the point where writers can make a decent living on it—and the world is still here.

We are in the frenzy phase for A.I.. There are no rules as to how ChatGPT should be used and for what. We only have the pioneers who are showing us what’s possible and the doomsday naysayers who are predicting the end of humanity. I believe we will land somewhere in the middle, though it will take some time. In the meantime, it is up to each one of us how we will use this amazing tool.

In this chapter, you’re going to see different ways to use ChatGPT in your fiction writing—from idea generating to drafting and editing. Later in the book (Chapter 6), you’ll see the principles of creating and structuring prompts to get the responses you are seeking.

My Journey with ChatGPT

I’ve been working on my debut novel The Call to Witchery since the idea popped into my head in 2016 and wouldn’t leave. When the first draft was done, I joined an online group and submitted a chapter each week for critiques. At the end of that, I felt it was still missing something and hired a writing coach to teach me the craft of writing. (As a technologist, I live by rules and standards. In my mind, the same had to exist for fiction.) He did, and we got the book to the point where it could be submitted to an editor—Alice Sudlow, the wonderful editor who I mentioned in the Introduction.

She praised the external story. It is engaging, action-packed and draws the reader in. However, the internal story was somewhat chaotic. There were character arcs, but they were not progressive and could be hard to identify.

To help me structure the internal character plot, she asked me to do the following:

a. Identify the value shifts (both internal and external) in an action fantasy story where the protagonist (in this case, a witch) moves from self-rejection to reluctant self-acceptance;

b. Using these value shifts, identify the stages of growth a witch would go through;

c. In these stages, identify the stages of grief the witch would experience in order to get to self-acceptance. Be sure to include all emotions felt at every stage;

d. Identify all of the emotional stages the witch would go through on Joseph Campbell’s Hero’s Journey;

e. Using the information above, map out how a witch would progress from self-rejection to reluctant self-acceptance of the magic which resides in her.

Plot

A story is a sequence of events which occur from the beginning to the end and tell the reader what is happening within them. A plot, on the other hand, tells us how those events are connected and why the story unfolded the way it did.

Whether you’re using three-, four-, or five-act structures, ChatGPT is familiar with all of them. Its default is the three-act structure. I found it useful to tell it what structure to use when asking for assistance. It will return information based on your preferences.

Both plotters and pansers will find the tool to be of use in relation to the plot that they either plan or develop as they write. I’m a plotter. I like the way Polly can give me a complete plot, starting with basic information and then building in depth as ideas occur to me.

Pantsers will find ChatGPT’s ability to come up with new ideas for the next part of the story useful, whether or not the ideas are ultimately incorporated into the novel. Through the skills of iterative prompting (covered in chapter 6), ChatGPT can develop nuances for each scene, all while following the structures you prefer.

A quick process to develop a plot with ChatGPT goes like this:

a. Begin with a genre or theme, such as “Help me brainstorm a plot for a science fiction story.” Once ChatGPT offers an initial concept that seems interesting, you can build upon it.

b. Refine the plot by asking it to add twists, complications, or subplots. For example, “I have a sci-fi plot about space exploration. Can you suggest some unexpected twists or conflicts?”

c. Fine-tune the plot by asking it to incorporate character-driven events, ensuring that conflicts and resolutions align with the overall story. For example, let’s say you have a character called Jane who is timid and avoids confrontations at all costs. Your character-driven prompt might look like this—“Jane has decided to confront her domineering boss about a recurring issue. Please weave up to this point, Jane’s internal growth and decision-making that leads to this point of the story.”

Character

Stories are about characters—their personalities, backstories, motivations, quirks, flaws, faults, values, and strengths. They are us, or a version of us we would like to be. Creating characters who are genuine, nuanced, and engaging is one of the pillars in storytelling that, if done well, can catapult a story from average to extraordinary—one that readers will recommend to all of their friends and read more than once.

Just like a story, there are elements to creating a character: physical description, background, personality, relationships, words and actions, motivations, and change. All characters in an engrossing story will have a character sheet detailing each of these elements. This is where ChatGPT can cut down the amount of time it takes to define the characters.

As with the plot, any information about the character you’ve already developed can be incorporated into what ChatGPT will produce. Start with a general prompt and, with each response, continue to ask for more specific information.

Setting

We writers are world builders, creating a new reality with every story we write. It doesn’t matter whether it is a corner of our current world or a magical alternate world accessible by portal or a spaceship in space. When we build it, we need to consider all aspects of the world (setting), including its geography, history, cultures, societies, technologies, magic systems (if applicable), and more. The goal of worldbuilding is to make the fictional world feel coherent, consistent, and vivid, enhancing the reader’s or viewer’s immersion in and understanding of the story’s context.

The good news is, Polly / Luna is already familiar with all of the elements that make up an interesting and diverse world. For example, ChatGPT is able to imagine different political systems that govern a society and then build the rules that govern it.

c. Ensure consistency by discussing the setting’s impact on characters and the story. You might ask, “How can I integrate the town’s history into the narrative?”

Conflict

Conflict, the essential heartbeat of every tale, keeps stories vibrant and readers engaged. It can manifest internally, where characters grapple with their own doubts and desires, or externally, as they face challenges from others or their surroundings.

While internal conflicts delve into emotional and psychological depths, external ones often propel the action and shape the plot. Without conflict, stories stagnate. It’s the suspense, the tension, the unresolved issues that compel readers to turn the page. In essence, weaving meaningful conflicts into a narrative ensures it resonates, lingers, and captivates.

Let’s not forget that without conflict, the character arc cannot occur. Conflict creates a well-rounded arc and unforgettable narrative.

Keeping with the process of moving from general to specific, try these steps:

a. Begin with the central conflict by stating the genre or context, like “Help me create a conflict for a romance novel.” ChatGPT will then propose initial conflicts for you to explore.

b. Expand on the conflict by discussing character motivations, obstacles, and stakes. For example, “In my fantasy story, how can I make the conflict between two kingdoms more complex?”

c. Continue prompting to ensure that the conflicts align with character growth and resolution throughout the story, maintaining a coherent narrative.

Point of View (POV)

You can spot POV if you ask yourself, “Who’s telling the story, and who are they telling it to?” Choosing the right POV will bring your story to life for the reader.

POV is split into three categories: a) first person—one of the characters (using “I”) is narrating the story; b) second person—the narrator is talking directly to the reader by using the pronouns “you,” “your,” and “yours”; and c) third person—which is subsequently subdivided into limited (sticking to one character’s range of perspective), and omniscient (knowing all characters’ thoughts and actions).

This is a huge area where ChatGPT can help save you time. By giving it a general outline of your story, you can ask it to write a scene in the first person POV of the protagonist. Follow that up by asking it to write the same scene from the second person POV, etc. In the end, you will have a good representation of how the different points of view play out so you can decide which one will resonate with your reader.

Other ways ChatGPT can help you:

a. Offering suggestions based on your preferred POV or context, like “I’m writing a first-person narrative. Can you offer tips on maintaining consistency?”

b. Switching or experimenting with different POVs to see how they affect the story. For example, “What would a third-person limited POV bring to my character-driven novel?”

c. Ensuring that your chosen POV aligns with the story’s goals and allows readers to connect with characters effectively.

We encounter symbols in daily life, from road signs to emojis, continuously communicating emotions, ideas, or warnings to us. In essence, a symbol can be almost anything—a mark, image, name, or place—that represents something more, acting as a bridge to deeper understanding.

Whether it’s through colors, animals, or even concepts like love and rebirth, symbols enrich and deepen a story. They’re the subtle nudges that suggest underlying themes and moods, enhancing storytelling by imbuing it with layers of meaning.

Foreshadowing

Whether you’ve been gripped by a novel that leaves you hungry for the next revelation or watched a movie where ominous moments leave clues about a character’s fate, you’ve experienced foreshadowing at work. Sometimes it can be blatant; other times, it’s subtle, only truly making sense when readers connect the dots in retrospect.

Foreshadowing serves several purposes, with the most prevalent being to heighten narrative suspense. It’s woven through dialogues, shifts in setting, or even the title of a work. Think of it as a storyteller’s way of setting or defying the audience’s expectations, making even the most unexpected events seem plausible. For instance, in a crime novel, a seemingly innocent conversation might hint at a character’s dark past, or in a romance, a fleeting glance could foreshadow a future relationship.

Essentially, foreshadowing plays on the distinction between the plot, the sequence of events, and the story. It’s dropping hints early without necessarily revealing the exact outcomes—teasing that something significant will unfold in the plot. This element can be found punctuating the ends of chapters or pivotal moments, particularly in suspenseful genres like Gothic novels or horror films. By carefully placing these cues, writers ensure readers are continuously engaged, eager to unravel the story’s mysteries.

ChatGPT can guide you toward better foreshadowing by:

a. Suggesting subtle hints and clues when you specify a future event or revelation you’d like to foreshadow, such as “I want to foreshadow a plot twist involving a hidden family secret.”

b. Discussing ways to integrate foreshadowing elements seamlessly into the narrative, ensuring they don’t feel forced or overly obvious. For instance, “How can I drop hints about the family secret without giving it away too soon?”

c. Evaluating the effectiveness of foreshadowing choices by considering reader reactions and ensuring that they enhance the story’s impact and surprise.

Narrative Structure and Techniques

In the world of storytelling, the narrative structure is the backbone, the architecture, that gives a tale its shape and direction. Think of it as the blueprint for your story, laying out the sequence of events and the relationship between those events. It’s the thread that weaves disparate elements together, providing coherence and meaning.

Narrative structure is not just about ordering events chronologically; it’s about choosing which events to highlight, which to downplay, and how to reveal them for maximum impact. It dictates the rhythm of a story—when to speed up the action, when to allow for a quiet moment of reflection, and when to deliver that twist that keeps readers riveted.

Now, narrative techniques are the specific storytelling tools and methods employed within the overarching framework of a narrative structure to effectively deliver the story’s content and engage the audience. Techniques like flashbacks, shifting perspectives, and stream-of-consciousness narration can be deftly woven into a narrative structure to enrich storytelling. For instance, flashbacks in a three-act structure might provide essential backstory in Act 1, intensify conflict in Act 2, and offer resolution in Act 3. Shifting perspectives can expand the scope in each act, allowing for a multi-faceted view of the story’s events. Stream-of-consciousness can deepen character development, particularly in moments of crisis or climax, offering a raw and immersive glimpse into a character’s inner world.

Both of these pieces set clear expectations for the reader, guiding them through the ebb and flow of the plot. By integrating flashbacks and foreshadowing within a compelling structure, the pacing and tension are meticulously controlled, captivating the reader’s attention from the inciting incident to the denouement. Additionally, employing multiple viewpoints and an omniscient narrator can illuminate complex tales, ensuring that the audience remains oriented and connected, even when navigating through non-linear timelines and layered subplots.

Incorrect or Nonsensical Information

If you haven’t yet, you will certainly experience nonsense as you work with ChatGPT. This comes about because of its core design, which utilizes algorithms to assemble text.

An algorithm, in the computer world, is a sequence of actions performed on data. You can think of it like a recipe. Within ChatGPT, Polly / Luna have been trained on huge amounts of text to recognize and generate language, allowing it to craft answers based on patterns it has learned. It doesn’t “search” the web in real-time like Google, but instead relies on its prior training to generate responses. Because Luna is using pattern recognition, she doesn’t know if the information she gave you is factually accurate or not.

Google uses a complex system to sift through billions of web pages to find and rank the most relevant ones for your search query. It considers many factors, like how often certain words appear, the quality of the website, and how other sites link to it.

While both Google and ChatGPT process vast amounts of information, Google is more like a librarian fetching you the best book from a library, whereas ChatGPT is like a well-read friend giving you an answer based on what they know.

An excellent example of how ChatGPT provides incorrect information comes from the writer Harry Guinness*. In the article, he gives an example of the response ChatGPT gave him when he queried “Harry Guinness is…” then compared the answer from the free ChatGPT and the paid model.

OpenAI is already making improvements in the accuracy of the data, but even so, you need to be vigilant with fact-checking any time you receive a detailed response.

ChatGPT’s responses are highly sensitive to the inputs it receives. Small alterations in the wording, tone, or context of a prompt can lead to significantly different responses. Even the same prompt repeated may lead to a variation of the initial response.

Again, this is the result of its design. It is not duplicating the response. It is making new calculations. Memory Limitations

ChatGPT has a limited ability to retain text in its “working memory.” That means, for longer or complex pieces of work or prompts with multiple interrelated questions, it might lose the contextual continuity, impacting the consistency of its responses.

How much is a “certain” amount? GPT-3, the model on which ChatGPT is based, has a context window of 2,048 tokens. Tokens can range from one character to one word, but on average in English, one token is roughly equivalent to 1.3 words. Therefore, in terms of words, the limit of the working memory is approximately 3,072 words, which can vary based on the complexity and structure of the text.

GPT-4 (paid model) has a bigger memory at approximately 64,000 words. What happens when you reach this limit? It gets total amnesia of everything before that–which includes the original question if that is what you started the chat with.

What does this mean for you? Be as clear and specific as possible each time you submit a prompt. Repeat anything that is important for ChatGPT to “remember” if your conversations are getting long. Summarize as best you can anything that will require a long explanation or break it up in chunks. Guide the conversation by giving explicit instructions or asking more questions. ChatGPT can be wordy when it responds, so this will help to keep it on focus. Also, you can give it a maximum word length for its responses.

Prompt Types

Conversing with ChatGPT is much like fiction writing—the words you choose craft the narrative and direction of the exchange.   3. Instruction-based: These prompts provide specific instructions for ChatGPT to follow. For instance, “Translate the following English text into French...”
  4. Opinion and Perspective: A prompt to provide an opinion or perspective, generally based on a hypothetical construct like, “What are the potential benefits of a four-day work week?”

  6. Step-by-step Explanation: A request for a detailed, sequential explanation or instruction, like “Explain the steps of photosynthesis.”

  10. Feedback: A request for constructive criticism or feedback on a piece of writing, like, “Can you provide feedback on this introduction to my essay?”
  11. Text Modifications: A request to modify, edit, or rephrase a given text, such as, “Can you make this sentence sound more formal?”
  12. Data Extraction: Instructing ChatGPT to pull specific information or data from a larger piece of text, like, “What are the main points of this article?”

  14. Excel Formulas: Asking ChatGPT to turn a desired outcome in Excel into a formula you can insert. For example, “Write an Excel formula to calculate the sales tax of the monetary value in column F, at a 6 percent rate.”

THE KEY TO CHATGPT

This is where it all comes together and everything we’ve discussed starts to make sense: how to build a prompt. Be aware there is no right way or wrong way to build a prompt. There’s only the measurement of, “Did I get what I wanted?”

Still unsure on how to approach interacting with ChatGPT? This might help. Have you ever stumbled upon a fascinating discovery and attempted to explain it at a dinner party? As you animatedly dive into details, you miss the glazed look on your friend’s face. Only when you pause to take a breath do you see their confusion. They might offer you a drink or, if you’re lucky, ask about the one thing they grasped. That’s your cue to simplify and share bit by bit instead.

Guess what? It’s the same with ChatGPT. If you give it too much information all at once, it will respond—but probably not with what you’re expecting. If you take the same approach as you would with your friend at the dinner party and feed it information in digestible chunks instead, you will find your interactions to be satisfying and productive. Just like your friend, ChatGPT will thank you.

The process outlined in this chapter is the same one I’ve alluded to throughout the book: start simple and add complexity a bit at a time.

At the beginning of the book, I discussed how I used ChatGPT to help me build out the internal emotional journey of a character. I started with the question of what stages a person might go through from self-rejection to self-acceptance. Then I followed up by asking it to incorporate the seven stages of grief. Which then led to including the emotional stages if a person were on the heroine’s journey. Finally, I asked it to introduce the theoretical psychotherapy framework to move from self-rejection to self-acceptance. By building out this framework in stages—rather than just asking it to deliver the final outcome in one prompt—I ended up with an outline that can be used as a guide to the internal growth of a character on the heroine’s journey.

Piecing It All Together

The steps below are a guide for your interactions with ChatGPT. As you use it more and more, you’ll find your own way to work with it. For example, you may not always start with simple, straightforward questions because you’re familiar with how much context to give when you start a new chat.

Although I am already familiar with ChatGPT, this is a process I follow over and over again until I get the results I want.

  1. Start Simple: Ask a simple, straightforward question or instruction, such as “List the top ten movies of 2020” or “Define anthropomorphic” or “Summarize the plot of (your favorite book).” Start with something you can fact-check on Google (remembering that Google isn’t always accurate either) until you’re confident in the responses you are getting back. I highly recommend this step in order to set your expectations of ChatGPT.

    Prompt structure: [Prompt command] + [simple text].

    Example: “Summarize” + “weather patterns.”

  2. Add Specificity: Once you’re comfortable with simple prompts, it’s time to get more specific. Start by adding descriptive statements to the original question, such as, “List the top ten highest-grossing movies of 2020 worldwide” or “Acting as a writer, how would you use the work ‘anthropomorphic’ in a scene” or “Explain the central theme of (your favorite book).” Remember that ChatGPT will provide you with answers in the format you requested, so the movies will come in a list, a scene, or a discussion based on the language you used. Again, I suggest you fact-check the information provided.

    Prompt structure: [Prompt command] + [specific area or topic] + [specific question or request].

    Example: “Define” + “quantum physics” + “in layman’s terms.”

  3. Provide Context: If you’re looking to get more tailored answers from ChatGPT, it helps to paint a fuller picture with your questions. With the movie example, a contextualized prompt could be “List the top ten highest-grossing movies of 2020 worldwide, and provide a brief synopsis for each.” For the usage of “anthropomorphic,” a prompt with context might be, “Acting as a writer depicting a scientist speaking to laymen, use the word ‘anthropomorphic’ in a scene. Be sure to include details to give the reader a sense of what the word means.” For the book theme, it might look like, “Explain the central theme of (your favorite book) and include a comparison to other books of the same genre written the same year.”

    Prompt structure: [Prompt command] + [specific area or topic] + [specific question or request and context to add to the question].

    Example: “Explain” + “in the context of the Renaissance period” + “the significance of Leonardo da Vinci’s works.”

  4. Indicate Desired Format: Now, give ChatGPT instructions on how you want to see the response. Keeping with the movie, the revised prompt could be, “Provide a bulleted list of the top ten highest-grossing movies of 2020 worldwide, and for each, write a one-sentence synopsis.” With this one sentence, we’ve given it the specific information we’re looking for and how we we want the answer formatted. Other formats examples are numbered list, table, FAQ, outline, flowcharts/diagrams (see previous chapter for a more complete list of additional formats).

    Prompt structure: [Prompt command] + [question or request] + [desired format]

    Example: “List” + “the main causes of the French Revolution” + “in bullet points.”

  5. Ask for Reasoning or Further Details: Asking ChatGPT to give you the reasons why it chose that response will give you more complex responses, which you can then use to give to your readers. For example, you could word it this way: “Provide a bulleted list of the top ten highest-grossing movies of 2020 worldwide, write a one-sentence synopsis for each, and explain why you think each film was successful” or “Present a bulleted list of ten books with the same theme as (your favorite book) and provide a synopsis of each. Explain why you’ve included each book.”

    Prompt structure: [Prompt command] + [question] + [request for explanation or details],

    Example: “Compare” + “photosynthesis and cellular respiration” + “highlighting their primary differences.”

    Iterate and Experiment: Prompt iteration involves refining a question or request in order to obtain a more precise or desired answer. Let’s explore this concept using “A Discovery of Witches” as an example. Through a series of prompts, I guided ChatGPT to delve into the book starting with what the book is about and leading to how ‘forbidden love’ is incorporated into it. This is how I did it:

    Prompt structure: [Prompt command or question] + [follow-up or iteration based on previous response]

    Example: “Describe” + “the water cycle,” followed by “Elaborate on the evaporation process” after ChatGPT responds.

The examples and templates provided here are enough to get you started. As you work more and more with it, you will find you are able to increase the complexity of your prompts.

I interact with Polly / Luna as my assistant. My prompts range from simple instructions to more nuanced and complex instructions.

When developing the introduction to the Story Elements section, I started with this:

Me

Taking all of the elements which we discussed above, can you please write an introduction to the section called Story Elements? It should be no more than 250 words

The answer I got was very close to what I wanted but still required tweaking, so I did that myself.

Again, there’s no one-size-fits-all formula when it comes to ChatGPT, only the simple question of, “Did I get what I wanted?”

Just as we writers build a character’s depth layer by layer, you can refine your prompts to adjust for specificity, context, format, and depth to generate increasingly complex responses.

As you continue experimenting with prompts and exploring ChatGPT’s capabilities, remember that it’s your assistant in the creative process, here to help you unlock new possibilities in your writing journey. So, start with a question, embrace simplicity, and unleash your creativity.

CHAPTER 8

BALANCING HUMAN CREATIVITY AND A.I. ASSISTANCE

Back in 2022, the introduction of ChatGPT marked a significant leap in computer capabilities. No longer confined to mere search engines, computers could now construct logical and grammatically sound sentences. The ability to engage in typed conversations and generate novel responses exposed the broader public to the progress companies had quietly been making over decades. This evolution is akin to an author who, wrongly labeled an overnight sensation, has actually honed their craft for years.

Soon after, organizations worldwide began banning ChatGPT, citing concerns about security, privacy, copyright, plagiarism, and data accuracy. These concerns are valid, but they largely pertain to human usage rather than the tool itself. Just as with any emerging technology, it will take time to establish laws and regulations that govern ethical A.I. use. Even then, there will always be individuals who exploit it for personal gain.

A good example of technology being used for nefarious reasons is the telephone. We’ve all been the recipients of calls made by scammers requesting money, access to our bank accounts, or credit card information. They can sound legitimate. And there have been many people who have lost money. But we have not banned the telephone. Instead, laws and regulations have been put into place to find and prosecute the scammers.

I expect that eventually the same will occur with ChatGPT and A.I.. In the meantime, some libraries, writer organizations, bookstores are banning AI-generated books (though in most cases, no definition of what this means has been provided) without fully comprehending that A.I. cannot replace the author. The irony is that these organizations are using A.I. tools to determine which books to ban, some of which were written before A.I. was here.

In addition to the limitations we’ve already talked about, we are going to examine the limitations of A.I. in the realm of story-telling. Yes, when prompted well, it can string together words in an entertaining and apropos way. But it still misses the mark on the nuances of craft—where we humans excel because of our unique combination of brainpower, creativity and imagination, emotional make-up and social know-how. Our ability to weave all that together is beyond anything A.I. can produce. It doesn’t have the life experiences we do.

Humans vs. A.I.

Skill

Let me be clear on this: Chat GPT can produce grammatically correct sentences. It can craft good descriptions. What it lacks is the ability to craft clever and original sentences. It follows grammar rules explicitly. But it doesn’t ever break them. You’ll never get sentences that begin with “and” or are written to be a stylistic fragment. In my experience, especially when I was working to draft many blog posts in one sitting, all of ChatGPT’s content started to sound the same after a while, because it all followed built-in rules with no deviation.

Context

ChatGPT can sometimes misinterpret context, especially in complex scenarios. For instance, while we humans can easily catch subtle nuances in a conversation due to shared cultural or societal knowledge, it will most likely might miss those cues. If someone mentions “It’s raining cats and dogs,” we instantly recognize it as an idiom indicating heavy rain. But ChatGPT? It might interpret it literally and so rather than the scene having the protagonist getting wet, ChatGPT will have them getting pummeled by animals raining down from the heavens.

Emotions

The richness of human emotions is something ChatGPT struggles to grasp fully. We might feel a pang of nostalgia from the smell of an old book or the sound of a childhood song, but for ChatGPT, these sensations and memories mean nothing. If you ask about the feeling of holding a loved one’s hand, it can only provide a programmed response, devoid of the genuine emotional depth we experience.

Problem-Spotting and Solving

When confronted with a problem, humans often draw upon a mix of past experiences, gut feelings, and analytical thinking. A.I., on the other hand, relies on the data it’s been fed. So, if it hasn’t been trained on a specific situation, its ability to come up with innovative solutions is limited.

Meaning and Subtext

Take a classic like Moby Dick. To us, it’s not just about a whale and a captain. It dives deep into themes of obsession, the nature of good and evil, and man’s struggle against fate. ChatGPT can summarize the plot, but grasping the profound layers and interpretations? No, because it lacks the ability to see beyond the words.

Connecting Disparate Topics

The human mind has a unique ability to see connections where they might not obviously exist. We can link the rhythm of a song to a heartbeat or find parallels between the flow of water in a river and the journey of life. Such abstract connections, born out of our varied web of experiences and knowledge, are beyond the scope of ChatGPT’s capabilities. Remember, ChatGPT was trained on existing internet data. It is incapable of experiencing the world and making connections of things that are seen and experienced outside of the computer world.

Spontaneous Generation of Thoughts and Ideas

Ever had an “a-ha” moment in the shower? Or a brilliant idea during a casual chat? That’s the spontaneity of the human mind at work, generating ideas when least expected. In contrast, ChatGPT responses are based on patterns and data and therefore are predetermined from the outset.

Human Language

In storytelling, our human language brings a rich, personal touch that evolves and resonates with raw emotions. It’s packed with our history and the unique flavors of different cultures, which make characters and their stories feel real. For all their precision and efficiency, computer languages fall short in capturing the essence of human sentiment and the different shades of meaning that give stories their heartbeat.

Complexity

The intricate mesh of emotions, experiences, instincts, and logic that form our decisions is a testament to the complexity of the human mind. From the arts to the sciences, our multifaceted approach to meaning-making and creativity is what has propelled our civilizations forward. Computers and A.I. have not yet reached that capability.

Responsible Use of A.I.

There is a delicate equilibrium to be found between A.I.’s capabilities and our innate human strengths. I believe finding that equilibrium within a responsible use of A.I. can foster a world that thrives on harmony, efficiency, and enrichment.

While A.I. incorporates inherent checks and balances, our responsibility extends to augmenting its use with our own set of ethical guidelines. This balance encompasses compliance with existing laws and the establishment of new regulations to safeguard individuals’ rights. However, it’s equally imperative to employ our own moral compass when navigating the A.I. landscape.

In light of these considerations, I made a conscious decision not to employ ChatGPT for editing or scene creation in my debut novel, The Call to Witchery. As a budding novelist, my primary objective is to maximize my book’s chances of success, steering clear of any potential bans stemming from A.I. involvement in its creation. Nevertheless, as detailed in chapter 4, I’ve harnessed ChatGPT for research and writing craft assistance in order to bolster my editing endeavors.

The rationale for this approach extends beyond risk factors. I am in the continual process of honing my craft, and ChatGPT, while a proficient tool, doesn’t assume the role of a teacher. It may rephrase, tweak, or reword based on its knowledge, but it lacks the capacity to challenge me as effectively as my human editor. Her keen insights have swiftly pinpointed areas of improvement in my manuscript, facilitating my efforts to craft a captivating internal emotional narrative—a facet of storytelling where ChatGPT falls short.

THE FUTURE OF A.I. IN FICTION WRITING

Incredible, isn’t it? In a realm where technology and creativity converge, artificial intelligence emerges as a true game-changer for fiction writers. Thanks to Polly / Luna, writers can now harness the boundless potential of A.I. to spark their imagination, streamline their creative process, and unlock entirely new dimensions of storytelling. These tools aren’t just mere assistants; they are creative partners in the authorial journey, offering unparalleled support in brainstorming, drafting, editing, and refining. The future of fiction writing has arrived, and it brims with exhilarating possibilities, where human ingenuity converges with A.I.’s infinite promise.

Throughout this book, I’ve embarked on a journey of discovery with these AI-powered tools. At the outset, I followed the content creation process pioneered by Jon Morrow in his blogging course, employing ChatGPT to outline and compose the content. Armed with the initial title of ChatGPT for Fiction Writers, I enlisted ChatGPT’s assistance to craft the book’s structure and contents, supplemented by my own ideas. This process spanned an entire weekend.

The next step was to read it through and edit to make it sound like me. This included the addition of stories and is where the limitations of ChatGPT came to the forefront. It doesn’t have the life experience to include the stories that bring words to life—even non-fiction words.

With the first draft done, I contacted an editor who specializes in non-fiction books. I am so glad I did. While the basic structure was used, things were rearranged, holes in the book were identified, and places where more information was needed were noted. I used ChatGPT to address some of these revisions. It gave me ideas on how to word things, acted as my research assistant, and provided more details to general points. In every instance, I personally edited what Polly / Luna provided. I can guarantee you that, without Polly / Luna, you wouldn’t be reading this book at this time because it wouldn’t be finished yet. I can also guarantee that you wouldn’t be reading the book Polly / Luna would have generated on their own, because the human touch was missing from everything they drafted. It sounded the way a machine would write—grammatically correct and lacking any flair.

I am looking forward to what the future holds for us writers. In the short time I’ve been working with ChatGPT, OpenAI introduced Custom Instructions, allowing ChatGPT to tailor its responses to you. For a new chat, they’ve introduced templates like “create a workout plan” or “brainstorm names (for an orange cat we’re adopting from the shelter)” to get you started. They’re also working on allowing Luna access to the latest information on the web. Now they’ve introduced the capability for us to design our own templates and make them available to other writers.

New developments in A.I. will create new opportunities, as well as challenges, for writers. Here are a few areas to keep an eye on:

  1. Improved contextual understanding: Current A.I. models like ChatGPT already have impressive capabilities for generating coherent and contextually relevant text. However, they are occasionally prone to drift off-topic or miss subtle nuances. Future iterations of these models are expected to offer far superior contextual understanding, enabling them to maintain consistent narratives more effectively and to more accurately interpret complex prompts.

    Implication for writers: An A.I. tool with better contextual understanding can be a more effective writing partner, assisting you in developing intricate plots and complex characters with greater consistency and depth.

  2. Advanced emotion and sentiment analysis: Future A.I. systems are likely to have more sophisticated abilities to understand and generate text with specific emotional tones. This could allow the A.I. to adapt its writing style to better match or complement the mood of your story, adding a new layer of expressiveness.

    Implication for writers: With a more nuanced understanding of emotion in text, A.I. could provide valuable feedback on the emotional impact of your writing or generate emotionally charged content that resonates deeply with your readers.

  3. Customizable A.I. personalities: In the future, you might be able to customize the “personality” of your A.I. tool, adjusting its writing style, tone, and preferred vocabulary to align with your needs or those of your project.

    Implication for writers: A customizable A.I. can be adapted to suit your unique writing voice or emulate the styles of different characters, enhancing your ability to create distinct narrative voices within your work.

  4. Integration with other A.I. technologies: As A.I. continues to progress, we can expect increased integration between different A.I. technologies. For example, A.I. models that generate visual content could work in tandem with language models like ChatGPT.

    Implication for writers: This could open up entirely new avenues for storytelling, enabling writers to create multimedia narratives with AI-generated text, images, and perhaps even sound.

The road to these advancements will not be without hurdles, as issues related to data security, copyright, and ethical A.I. use will continue to challenge developers and users alike. However, the promise of A.I. in fiction writing is undeniable, offering the potential to enhance creativity, improve productivity, and open up unprecedented storytelling possibilities.
