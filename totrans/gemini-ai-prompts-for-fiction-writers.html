PROMPTS

Genre Benders:

  1. Give me ideas on how I can rewrite this classic fairy tale from the villain's perspective.

  2. Imagine a world where technology and magic exist side-by-side. Write a story set in this world.

  3. Combine two seemingly opposite genres (e.g., sci-fi romance, historical comedy) and create a story.

  4. Write a story where the ending contradicts the typical expectations of your chosen genre.

  5. Reimagine a famous historical event with a fantastical twist.

Character Prompts:

  1. Write a story about a character who can talk to animals.

  2. Develop a character who is haunted by a past mistake.

  3. Create a story where the protagonist and antagonist are forced to work together.

  4. Write a character who possesses an unusual talent or skill, and explore its impact on their life.

  5. Write a story about a character who wakes up with amnesia and must piece together their identity.

Storytelling Techniques:

  1. Start your story with a bang – a dramatic event that throws your protagonist into action.

  2. Write a scene entirely from the point of view of a non-human character.

  3. Challenge yourself to write a story using only dialogue.

  4. Tell your story through a series of letters, journal entries, or newspaper clippings.

  5. Write a story with a limited vocabulary (e.g., 100 words).

Worldbuilding Prompts:

  1. Create a world with a unique weather system (e.g., raining glass, sentient winds).

  2. Develop a society with a strict social hierarchy and explore its consequences.

  3. Imagine a world where a single resource is the key to power and survival.

  4. Write a story set in a city built on the back of a giant creature.

  5. Create a world where emotions have a physical manifestation.

Emotional Prompts:

  1. Write a story about a character facing a life-or-death situation.

  2. Explore the theme of betrayal through a character's journey.

  3. Write a story about a character overcoming their greatest fear.

  4. Craft a narrative about the power of love (or loss) between two characters.

  5. Explore the theme of hope and resilience in the face of adversity.

Plot Prompts:

  1. A mysterious object falls from the sky, forever changing the world.

  2. A forgotten prophecy resurfaces, leading to a dangerous quest for the truth.

  3. People's dreams start leaking into reality, causing chaos and confusion.

  4. A hidden message is discovered, leading to a thrilling treasure hunt.

  5. Technology grants humans immortality, but at a terrible cost.

Creative Exercises:

  1. Write a poem inspired by this piece of art: [link/details].

  2. Turn a common object into a character and write a story about its life.

  3. Take a news headline and write a fictional story based on it.

  4. Listen to a piece of music and write a story inspired by its mood and rhythm.

  5. Write a story using a random word generator as your starting point.

Character Development Prompts:

  1. Create a backstory for a minor character in an existing story.

  2. Write a scene showcasing a character's greatest strength.

  3. Explore a character's hidden weakness through their actions.

  4. Describe a pivotal moment that shaped a character's personality.

  5. Write a dialogue scene that reveals a character's deepest desires.

Flash Fiction Prompts:

  1. Write a story in exactly 100 words.

  2. The first sentence of your story must be: "The last thing I expected to find was..."

  3. Write a story with a surprise twist ending.

  4. The climax of your story takes place in an elevator.

  5. Write a story inspired by a single emotion (e.g., joy, fear, anger).

Bonus Prompts:

  1. Explore the concept of parallel universes in your writing.

  2. Write a story about artificial intelligence that becomes self-aware.

  3. Write a dystopian future where humanity faces a major threat.

  4. Reimagine a classic story with a modern twist.

  5. Write a story that leaves the ending open to interpretation.
