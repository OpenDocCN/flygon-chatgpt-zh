Glossary of AI Terms

AI (Artificial Intelligence): Machines designed to act and think like humans, including learning from experience and making decisions

AI ethics: The rules and principles guiding the responsible creation, use, and impact of AI technologies on society

AI-generated tool: Software that uses AI to automatically create content, solve problems, or generate data

AI-Powered world: A society where AI is part of everyday life, helping with jobs, entertainment, health care, and more

Algorithm: A step-by-step guide for AI to achieve something specific

Application Programming Interface (API): A set of rules and tools for building software and applications, allowing different programs to communicate with each other

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): AI with the ability to understand, learn, and apply knowledge across many tasks at a human level

Backpropagation: A training method for neural networks where errors are corrected backward to improve performance

Big data: Large sets of data that can be analyzed to find patterns, trends, and insights

Bias (AI context): When an AI makes unfair decisions based on its programming or training data

Chatbot: A program designed to simulate conversation with human users online

ChatGPT: An AI by OpenAI that can chat, answer questions, and provide information based on its training

Clarity: Being clear and easy to understand, important when instructing AI

Computer vision: AI that enables computers to understand and interpret visual information

Content creation: Making articles, videos, and posts to share online

Contextual understanding: AI’s ability to understand the background or situation of a question or task

Contextualization: Adding background information to give more meaning to a task for AI

Conversational AI: AI designed to interact with humans in a natural, human-like way

Creativity: Using imagination to come up with new and original ideas or content

Cross-Questioning: Asking follow-up questions to get more accurate information from AI

CustomGPT: A customized version of GPT AI for specific tasks or industries

DALL-E: An AI by OpenAI that creates images from text descriptions

Data augmentation: Increasing the diversity of data for training models without collecting new data

Deep learning: A type of machine learning using deep neural networks to analyze and learn from data

Effective communication with AI: Clearly conveying what you want AI to do for better responses

Equity: Ensuring fairness and justice in procedures and resource distribution

Federated learning: Training a model across many devices or servers holding local data samples

Generative AI: AI that can create new content like text, images, or music

GAN (Generative Adversarial Network): A machine learning framework where two networks compete to create realistic data

Google Gemini AI: A free web-based AI with image generation and live web browsing

GPT (Generative Pre-Trained Transformer): An AI that understands and generates text like a human

GPT store: A place in ChatGPT apps to find and use various AI tools and applications

Hallucinations (AI context): When an AI makes up information or presents false facts

Human-centered values: Principles focusing on human well-being and dignity in technology

Image recognition: AI’s ability to identify objects, places, people, and actions in images

Interactive elements: Parts of digital media users can interact with, like buttons or links

Iteration: Repeating a process until a desired outcome is achieved

Language model: An AI that understands, generates, and manipulates human language

Large language model (LLM): An advanced language model that uses large data sets to understand and generate human language accurately

Machine learning (ML): AI that allows computers to learn from data and improve over time

Microsoft Copilot App: A mobile app by Microsoft using GPT-4 for AI image generation and live web browsing

Natural language processing (NLP): Technology for computers to understand and interact with human language

Neural network: Algorithms that recognize relationships in data, similar to how the human brain works

Overfitting: When a model learns details and noise from training data too well, performing poorly on new data

Predictive analytics: Using data to predict future trends or events

Prompt: A question or instruction given to an AI for a specific response

Reinforcement learning: Learning where an AI agent makes decisions based on rewards or penalties

Robotics: Technology for designing, building, and using robots

Structured data: Information organized in a predefined way, often in databases

Supervised learning: Machine learning where the model is trained on labeled data

Token: A piece of text treated as a single data unit in AI

Training data: The data set used to teach an AI model

Transfer learning: Using a model developed for one task as a starting point for a different task

Turing test: A test to determine if a machine’s behavior is indistinguishable from a human’s

Unsupervised learning: Machine learning on unlabeled data to find hidden patterns

Voice recognition: Technology for devices to understand and respond to human speech.
