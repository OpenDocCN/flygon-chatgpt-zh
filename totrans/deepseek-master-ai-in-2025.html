To grasp tokenization, think of it as a way of breaking down text into smaller pieces, or “tokens”. DeepSeek, however, implements dynamic tokenization that adjusts the number and type of tokens depending on the content and complexity of the text it is processing. The ability to manage long context is crucial for the effectiveness of any conversational AI model. One of the most critical aspects of using DeepSeek is how we formulate our queries or prompts. Prompt engineering is a technique that optimizes communication with AI, ensuring that responses are more accurate and aligned with our needs.

What is a Prompt?

A prompt is the instruction we provide to DeepSeek to obtain a response. While a simple prompt might be something like “Explain the theory of relativity,” more complex tasks require a much higher level of specificity.

Strategies for Optimizing Prompts

To improve the quality of DeepSeek’s responses, we can apply the following strategies:

  • Contextualization: Add specific details about the context of the query.

  • Iteration: Refine the prompt based on the results obtained.

  • Defined Output Format: Indicate how we want the response (summary, table, list, etc.).

  • Use of Examples: Providing examples of the expected output can improve AI accuracy.

For example, instead of writing “Analyze this sales report,” a better prompt would be: “Analyze sales trends over the past three months in the Latin American region and generate a summary of the best-selling products.” This version directs the AI toward a more precise response.
