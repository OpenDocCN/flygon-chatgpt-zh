Chapter 1: Why AI eBooks Are a Passive Income Powerhouse

 

Imagine waking up to new deposits in your account—sales from a book you wrote last week, or even last year. That’s the power of eBooks , and thanks to AI, creating them is faster, cheaper, and easier than ever.

An eBook is simply a digital book—usually between 10 to 50 pages—focused on a specific topic, solution, or skill. You can sell it on platforms like Amazon KDP , Payhip , or Gumroad , and buyers can instantly download it to read on their phone, tablet, or Kindle. With just a single file, you can reach global readers , earn royalties for years, and never worry about inventory, shipping, or printing costs.

Why eBooks Are the Ultimate Digital Product

1. Low Cost
You don’t need a team of editors, expensive software, or a marketing agency. With ChatGPT , Canva , and free publishing platforms, you can write and launch a book for zero upfront cost .

2. Fast to Create
Unlike traditional publishing, which can take months, you can use AI to outline, write, and format an eBook in a weekend . Tools like ChatGPT write entire chapters in minutes, and Canva helps you design a professional cover in under an hour.

3. Evergreen and Scalable
Once published, your eBook can generate sales for years. And you’re not limited to one title—you can create a series , bundle titles together, or write on multiple topics. The more books you have, the more your income grows.

 

Income Potential from eBooks

  • 100 sales/month = $500/month
  • 200 sales/month = $1,000/month

Now imagine you have 5-10 books live—each generating a few sales daily. You’re suddenly looking at a serious stream of passive income .

And because platforms like Amazon and Payhip do the selling for you, your focus stays on creating great content, not handling transactions or customer support.

Chapter 2: Writing Your eBook with ChatGPT

A great eBook starts with clarity and structure . You don’t need to be an experienced writer or bestselling author to publish your first book. You just need an idea , a framework , and a reliable AI tool to help you write fast and clean. That’s where ChatGPT comes in.

This chapter walks you through using ChatGPT to brainstorm topics , outline chapters , generate content , and finish a polished eBook —often in just a single day.

 

Step 1: Brainstorming eBook Topics with ChatGPT

Start by asking ChatGPT to generate high-demand, trending topics .

Prompt: List 10 trending eBook topics for 2025.

You’ll get responses like:

  • Minimalist living
  • Passive income for beginners
  • AI tools for entrepreneurs
  • Digital detox strategies

Pick a topic that:

  • Excites you
  • Reflects something you’ve explored
  • Is easy to research or talk about

You don’t have to be an expert— ChatGPT fills the gaps , making it possible for anyone to write.

 

 

Step 2: Narrow Down to a Niche

Broad topics are harder to sell. To stand out, go niche-specific .

Examples:

  • Instead of “Productivity” → “Productivity Hacks for Remote Workers”
  • Instead of “AI” → “How to Use ChatGPT for Etsy Sellers”

Spend 5 minutes listing three focused variations of your chosen topic. Pick one that feels specific yet flexible —something you can build a short book around.

 

Step 3: Outline Your eBook with ChatGPT

Once your niche is clear, it’s time to structure your book .

Prompt: Create a five-chapter eBook outline for [your topic], with chapter titles and 2–3 bullet points for each chapter.

Example Outline for Minimalist Living:

Chapter 1: What Is Minimalism?
– The core philosophy
– Common myths
– The benefits of simplicity

Chapter 2: Decluttering Your Space
– The 80/20 rule in action
– How to decide what to keep
– Creating open, calm environments

This structure gives you a clear table of contents and a roadmap to follow when writing. Save it in Google Docs or wherever you’ll build your book.

 

 

Step 4: Write Each Chapter Using ChatGPT

Now you’re ready to generate your content .

Prompt: Write a 600-word chapter on [Chapter Title], covering the following points: [Insert bullet points].

ChatGPT will create your first draft in seconds. Read through and lightly edit for:

  • Tone
  • Flow
  • Accuracy
  • Personal touches

You can also use follow-up prompts like:

  • Make this more casual.
  • Add a motivational call-to-action at the end.
  • Simplify this for a 9th grade reading level.

Repeat this for each chapter. With 5 chapters , you’ll typically have a 3,000–4,000 word eBook by the end of the day.

 

Step 5: Final Edits and Polishing

Once your chapters are written, do a quick pass for grammar and readability . You can use tools like:

  • Grammarly (free)
  • Hemingway Editor
  • Google Docs spell check

Make sure:

  • Chapter titles are consistent
  • Tone matches your audience
  • It’s easy to read on phones and tablets

Optional: Ask ChatGPT to write your Introduction and Conclusion .

Prompt: Write a 100-word introduction for an eBook about [your topic].

You now have a complete eBook draft : written by AI, edited by you, and ready to publish.

In the next chapter, we’ll show you how to design the cover, format the content, and publish your book on Amazon KDP or Payhip . You're halfway there—let’s keep the momentum going.

 

Chapter 3: Designing and Publishing Your eBook with Canva

With your eBook fully written, it’s time to make it look great and go live . You don’t need expensive tools or design experience—just Canva , ChatGPT , and free self-publishing platforms . This chapter walks you through designing a professional cover , formatting the interior pages , and publishing your book to platforms like Amazon KDP and Payhip .

You’ll walk away with a beautiful, ready-to-sell eBook in just a few hours.

Step 1: Designing Your eBook Cover in Canva

Your cover is your first impression —and a key factor in getting clicks and sales.

  • Go to canva.com and search “eBook Cover” in the template library.
  • Choose a clean, professional design that fits your topic.
  • Update the title , subtitle , and add your author name (Bruce Goldwell or pen name).
  • Use bold fonts, strong contrast, and minimal clutter.
  • For images, you can use Canva’s free elements or grab a Midjourney-generated image ($10/month) for something unique.

Export your finished cover as a high-quality PNG or JPEG .

Tip: Stick to a consistent design style if you plan to write multiple books in a series.

Step 2: Formatting the Interior

You have two easy formatting options:

  1. Canva – for PDF eBooks (best for Payhip and Gumroad)
  2. Google Docs / Word – for Amazon KDP

For Canva:

  • Open a new “A4 Document” in Canva.
  • Paste in your chapters one by one.
  • Use bold headers for chapter titles.
  • Choose easy-to-read fonts like Open Sans or Lato (12–14 pt).
  • Add occasional images or icons from Pexels or Canva's library.
  • Insert a title page, table of contents, and a short author bio at the end.

Export the entire book as a PDF when you're done.

For Google Docs:

  • Paste in your book content with simple formatting.
  • Add page breaks between chapters.
  • Use Heading 1 for titles and normal text for paragraphs.
  • Save as a .docx file for Amazon KDP or export as ePub using Reedsy.com (free tool).

Step 3: Publishing to Amazon KDP

If you want your eBook listed on the world’s biggest book marketplace, upload it to kdp.amazon.com . It’s free and takes less than 30 minutes.

Steps:

  • Go to KDP and sign in with your Amazon account.
  • Click “Create a Kindle eBook.”
  • Enter your title , author name , and book description .
  • Add up to 7 keywords related to your niche (use ChatGPT to help generate these).
  • Choose 2 categories that match your book topic.
  • Upload your manuscript (.docx or ePub) and your cover (JPEG or PNG).
  • Set your price ($2.99–$9.99 recommended for most eBooks).

Amazon pays 70% royalties on books priced between $2.99 and $9.99, so even 10–20 sales/month can add up quickly.

Step 4: Publishing to Payhip or Gumroad

If you want full control and instant payouts , publish your PDF eBook on Payhip or Gumroad .

For Payhip:

  • Go to payhip.com and sign up for a free account.
  • Click “Add Product” → “Digital Product.”
  • Upload your PDF eBook , enter a title, description, and price.
  • Add a cover image.
  • Hit publish and share your link anywhere online.

For Gumroad:

  • Sign up at gumroad.com .
  • Upload your book and set the price.
  • Gumroad takes ~10% in fees, but you keep the rest.
  • You can also activate affiliate support , letting others promote your eBook for a commission.

Tip: Gumroad and Payhip are ideal for direct sales through X, Pinterest, or email lists , while Amazon KDP gives you visibility to millions of readers.

Example Workflow Summary

Here’s a typical eBook production workflow using only free tools:

  • Write book with ChatGPT – 3 hours
  • Design cover with Canva – 30 minutes
  • Format interior in Canva or Docs – 1 hour
  • Publish to KDP and Payhip – 30 minutes

Total: Around 5 hours to go from idea to published book.

Once published, your book becomes a passive income asset , selling every day without additional work.

Chapter 4: Marketing and Scaling Your eBook Sales

Now that your eBook is live, it’s time to get it in front of buyers. Even the best-written book won’t sell unless people can find it. This chapter shows you how to use free AI tools and social platforms to drive traffic to your book, boost visibility, and turn a single eBook into a steady income stream.

You’ll learn how to optimize your listing, promote effectively, and scale your eBook business to $500 or more per month.

Optimize Your Title and Description with Keywords

Your eBook title and description are what make people click. To show up in Amazon, Payhip, or Gumroad search results, include specific keywords your audience is likely to use.

For your title, include the main benefit and a trending keyword. For example:
Minimalist Living: A Beginner’s Guide to Simplify Your Life in 2025

Keep it under 60 characters when possible, so it doesn’t get cut off in search listings.

Next, write a keyword-rich description using ChatGPT.

Prompt : Write a 100-word product description for a [topic] eBook with 3 key benefits.

Example output:
Struggling to simplify your space and mind? This guide teaches you how to embrace minimalist living in 2025. You’ll learn how to declutter with confidence, build better habits, and create more space for what matters. Includes actionable tips, a checklist, and a step-by-step plan. Start your minimalist journey today!

Make sure your description includes the target keyword (e.g., “minimalist living guide”) at least twice.

Promote on X, Pinterest, and Medium

Once your listing is optimized, promote it using free, fast platforms where your audience already spends time. Focus on X (Twitter) , Pinterest , and Medium .

On X
Post short teasers about your book, using relevant hashtags.

Example post:
Just published my new eBook! Learn how to simplify your space and your life. Now live on Amazon → [link] #MinimalistLiving #eBook #PassiveIncome

Use 2–3 hashtags per post. Post 2–4 times per week with variations—quotes, questions, benefits, or a behind-the-scenes look.

On Pinterest
Use Canva to create vertical pins (735x1102px) with your eBook title and a clean mockup of your cover.

Pin examples:

  • “Minimalist Living eBook – $5 Download”
  • “Declutter Your Life in 7 Days – Instant PDF Guide”

Post to boards related to self-help , digital products , or lifestyle hacks . Add a link to your Payhip or Gumroad product page.

On Medium
Repurpose part of your book into a short article (300–500 words). Add a CTA at the end linking to your book.

Title ideas:

  • “How I Simplified My Life in One Weekend”
  • “3 Decluttering Hacks from My New eBook”

Tip: You can ask ChatGPT to rewrite a chapter summary as a Medium post.

Repurpose Content with ChatGPT

Work smarter by turning one chapter into multiple content pieces using AI. Here's how:

Prompt : Turn this chapter on [topic] into 5 tweets under 280 characters.

Prompt : Write a Pinterest caption for this chapter (under 50 words).

Prompt : Create a 100-word blog post using this eBook excerpt.

This lets you create 5–10 unique posts per chapter in under 15 minutes. Use Buffer or Later to schedule everything in advance.

This simple repurposing process helps you maintain consistent promotion without spending hours each day.

Scale with More Books and Bundles

Once you’ve finished your first eBook, repeat the process for a related topic . This builds momentum and increases your chances of being found by more buyers.

Chapter 5: Turning One eBook into a Series or Bundle

Publishing your first eBook is just the beginning. The fastest way to grow your income is to build momentum by turning one successful title into a series , bundle , or even a digital library of related products. Readers who enjoy one book are much more likely to buy the next—especially if you make it easy and offer value.

Here’s how to scale your brand and multiply your income stream with just a few extra steps.

Create a Themed 3-Part Series

If your first book solves one problem, your second and third can dig deeper or expand into related topics. Example series:

  • Minimalist Living 1: Declutter Your Home
  • Minimalist Living 2: Declutter Your Mind
  • Minimalist Living 3: Minimalist Budgeting

Each book can be short and actionable—10 to 30 pages—and priced between $2.99 and $5. You’re building a micro brand inside your niche. Series titles work especially well on Amazon KDP and Gumroad, where readers tend to buy more from the same creator.

Bundle Multiple Short eBooks

Once you have two or more eBooks, bundle them into a higher-ticket digital product .

Example:
Three $5 eBooks can be bundled into a $15 PDF bundle on Payhip or Gumroad. You can also include extras like printable checklists, bonus guides, or a one-page action plan to boost the perceived value.

This not only increases your average order value , but it also gives you something to promote during special events or sales (e.g., “3-for-1 Limited Time Bundle”).

Cross-Promote Inside Each Book

At the end of every eBook, add a simple call-to-action linking to the next book or bundle.

Example:
Enjoyed this guide? Grab Part 2: Minimalist Living for the Mind—only $1.99 right here: [link]

Use the same cover branding across the series so they look like they belong together. Consistent visuals make you look more professional and trustworthy.

Add Bonuses to Increase Value

People love extras. Use Canva to create:

  • Printable worksheets
  • Checklists
  • Trackers
  • Quickstart guides

Then package them with your books or offer them as a bonus for buying the bundle. You could even offer a workbook version of the eBook that readers can print and fill out.

Why This Strategy Works

When you publish a single eBook, you get one chance to earn from each customer. When you publish a series and offer bundles , you increase:

  • Repeat buyers
  • Customer lifetime value
  • Your visibility on Amazon, Payhip, and Gumroad

More products = more shelf space = more chances to sell.

You don’t need to write long books. You just need to show up consistently , build around your niche, and give buyers more of what they already enjoy.
