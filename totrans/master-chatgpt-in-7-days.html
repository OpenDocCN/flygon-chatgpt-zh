Day 1 – Getting Started with ChatGPT

Objective: Create your free ChatGPT account and become familiar with the interface, app, and first basic interactions.

Why This Matters

Mastering ChatGPT begins with understanding where and how to access it. From the mobile app to the web interface, Day 1 is all about building confidence and fluency with the tool.

Step 1: Creating Your ChatGPT Account

  1. Visit the official website: https://chat.openai.com
     

  2. Click on Sign Up
  3. Register using your email or a Google/Apple/Microsoft account
  4. Verify your phone number
  5. You’ll land on the main ChatGPT interface

Step 2: Accessing ChatGPT on Mobile

  • iOS: https://apps.apple.com/app/openai-chatgpt/id6448311069
      

  • Android: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.openai.chatgpt

Step 3: Understanding the Interface

Explore these interface elements:

  • Prompt input box
  • Sidebar with history
  • Settings (GPT-3.5 / GPT-4 toggle, language, custom instructions)
  • Chat memory (if enabled)

Step 4: Your First Prompt

Try typing:

“Explain the difference between productivity and efficiency.”
“Give me 3 tips to improve my productivity at work.”

Day 2 – Prompt Engineering Fundamentals

Objective: Learn how to write better prompts to get smarter and more accurate results from ChatGPT.

Why This Matters

The quality of ChatGPT's responses depends heavily on the clarity and structure of your prompt. Today you’ll master the art of communicating effectively with the AI.

Step 1: What Makes a Prompt Effective?

Key principles of a good prompt:

  • Be specific
  • Provide context
  • State the format you want
  • Limit or guide the scope
  • Use examples when needed

Example:
“Summarize this paragraph in 3 bullet points for a presentation.”
“Write a polite customer service email apologizing for late delivery.”

Step 2: Try and Improve

Prompt #1:
“Tell me about marketing.”
Too vague.

Improved Prompt:
“Explain the basics of digital marketing to a beginner, using simple terms and a short list format.”
Precise, clear, and with intent.

Try this exercise:
Write a vague prompt. Then refine it using the 5 principles above.

Step 3: Use Format Instructions

You can ask ChatGPT to deliver its answers in:

  • Lists
  • Tables
  • Headings and subheadings
  • JSON or Markdown
  • Even role-based replies (e.g. “Answer as a tutor”)

“Give me a table comparing 3 email marketing tools by price and features.”
“Write an article draft in Markdown format for my blog.”

Step 4: Prompt Templates You Can Reuse

Save and re-use these universal prompt formulas:

  • “Act as a [role] and help me [goal]...”
  • “Explain [topic] to a [type of audience]...”
  • “Give me [number] ideas for [context]...”
  • “Turn the following into [format/output]...”

Tip: You can create a document or a note collection with your favorite prompts.

Day 3 – Crafting Effective Prompts for Advanced Use

Objective: Learn how to guide complex tasks through structured prompts, context chaining, and follow-up refinement.

Why This Matters

Complex tasks require more than a simple question. Today you’ll practice breaking big goals into smaller, clear instructions that ChatGPT can handle step-by-step.

Step 1: Structuring a Multi-Step Prompt

Example structure:

  1. Role: Define who ChatGPT should act as
  2. Goal: What you want to achieve
  3. Steps: Outline the process
  4. Format: Specify output layout

“Act as a productivity coach. Help me create a weekly plan by asking questions, then summarizing it in a bullet list.”

Step 2: Use Follow-Up Prompts

ChatGPT remembers context within a single session. You can:

  • Add clarification (“Make it shorter”)
  • Request changes (“Add emojis”)
  • Build on outputs (“Now turn this into a table”)
  • Ask “Why did you choose this?” to reveal reasoning

“That’s great! Now rewrite it with a fun tone and emojis.”
“Can you explain that last point further?”

This iterative approach lets you collaborate with ChatGPT like a real assistant.

Step 3: Use Constraints and Examples

Add clear rules or examples to shape responses:

  • “Keep it under 100 words”
  • “Use professional tone”
  • “Here’s an example – follow this format”
  • “Avoid using passive voice”
  • “Respond only in bullet points”

“Summarize this story like a TED Talk intro. Use suspense, avoid spoilers, be under 60 seconds.”

These signals help ChatGPT align with your expectations.

Step 4: Using the Memory (If Available)

ChatGPT Plus accounts may include memory features:

  • It remembers preferences between sessions
  • You can ask it to remember your writing style, goals, or tone
  • You can also turn memory off for privacy

To learn more, check: https://help.openai.com/en/articles/7730893

Day 4 – Automating Tasks with ChatGPT

Objective: Discover how to automate repetitive or time-consuming tasks using ChatGPT and reduce your daily workload.

Why This Matters

One of ChatGPT’s greatest strengths is automating small, repetitive tasks. Whether you're writing, organizing, or planning, ChatGPT can handle much of the heavy lifting with the right instructions.

Step 1: Identify Repetitive Tasks

Start by listing what you do frequently:

  • Writing emails
  • Summarizing meetings
  • Drafting social media posts
  • Reformatting documents
  • Creating checklists or SOPs

Pick 1 or 2 that you'd like to offload.

“Write a follow-up email thanking someone for a meeting and summarizing next steps.”

“Create a social media calendar with 5 posts a week, each with a caption and emoji.”

Step 2: Use Prompt Recipes

Turn your tasks into reusable prompts. Example:

“Act as my virtual assistant. Every Monday, generate a to-do list for the week, broken down by priority and deadlines.”

Create a text file or template library for:

  • Weekly reports
  • Brainstorming sessions
  • Email responses
  • Resume updates
  • Quick project briefs

Prompt building becomes automation when reused consistently.

Step 3: Combine ChatGPT with Other Tools

While ChatGPT itself doesn’t execute tasks, it can:

  • Generate text you paste into apps
  • Help you write Zapier/Make formulas
  • Draft Google Sheets formulas or Excel macros
  • Guide you in setting up automation platforms

“Create an email template with variables that I can use in a mail merge.”

“Help me build a Zapier workflow that posts LinkedIn updates automatically.”

Step 4: Save and Streamline Your Workflows

ChatGPT can document and optimize processes:

“Help me write an SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) for onboarding new clients.”

Ask it to:

  • Find inefficiencies
  • Organize steps
  • Suggest improvements
  • Format in plain English or bullet points

You’ve learned to prompt, structure, and automate. Now it’s time to push ChatGPT further: turn it into a teacher, an editor, a translator, a creative partner.

“Summarize this PDF into a slide outline.”

ChatGPT can:

Day 6 – Mastering Complex Queries

Objective: Learn how to get deeper, more refined results from ChatGPT by mastering long-form queries, logic, constraints, and multi-turn strategy.

Why This Matters

Simple questions are easy. But when your work requires layered reasoning, comparisons, or logic, you need advanced techniques. Today’s focus: precision and control.

Step 1: Use Clear Logical Structures

Break complex queries into parts:

  • Define the problem
  • Give background
  • Ask sub-questions
  • Request specific formats

“I’m starting a business. Based on the following criteria, suggest 3 cities: low taxes, tech talent, startup grants. Present the answer in a comparison table.”

Step 2: Work with Data and Constraints

Add logical rules:

  • “Exclude answers that contain X”
  • “Use only countries in Europe”
  • “Only include options under $100”
  • “List in descending order of popularity”

“Suggest productivity apps that don’t require a subscription and work offline. Rank by simplicity.”

“Here’s my daily schedule. Suggest time slots for focused work, avoiding 12–2PM.”

These filters increase relevance dramatically.

Step 3: Iterate With Precision

If the result isn’t quite right:

  • Say what was wrong:
    “Too long.” / “Too casual.” / “Missed point #2.”
  • Ask for specific fixes:
    “Add stats.” / “Use numbered list.” / “Cite sources.”

“Nice outline. Now turn it into a full article with an intro, conclusion, and real-world examples.”

Treat it like a real assistant who learns from feedback.

Step 4: Challenge GPT with Hypotheticals and Strategy

Push the model to explore scenarios or think strategically:

  • “If I had $1000 and wanted to market an ebook in 2 weeks, how would you approach it?”

  • “Create a step-by-step plan for someone who wants to become fluent in Spanish using free tools.”

  • “How could someone with only 5 hours a week build a side hustle with ChatGPT?”

These stretch prompts push the limits of the model's planning and insight.

Day 7 – Using ChatGPT to Boost Productivity and Success

Objective: Combine everything you’ve learned to build personal systems, routines, and tools with ChatGPT that improve your work, creativity, and focus.

Why This Matters

The real power of ChatGPT isn’t just in single-use tasks, but in helping you create sustainable workflows. Today you’ll build your personal AI-powered productivity system.

Step 1: Design a Daily AI Routine

ChatGPT can support your day from start to finish:

  • Morning planning: “Generate a to-do list based on these goals”

  • Focus sessions: “Give me a 25-minute Pomodoro with a checklist and motivating quote”

  • End-of-day review: “Summarize what I did and what I can improve tomorrow”

Step 2: Build Systems, Not Just Tasks

Turn one-off prompts into repeatable systems:

  • Meeting Note Taker
  • Email Assistant
  • Idea Tracker
  • Content Calendar Generator
  • Research Summary Builder
     

“Every Friday, help me summarize my week and write a reflection journal entry.”

“Help me evaluate weekly goals and set new ones.”

You can save these prompts in Notion, Obsidian, or any note-taking app.

Step 3: Integrate With External Tools

Use ChatGPT in combination with:

  • Google Sheets: ask for formulas or structure
  • Canva or Docs: generate raw content
  • Zapier/Make: automate trigger-based flows
  • Trello/Notion: build task cards or notes from prompts

Step 4: Track, Reflect, and Improve

ChatGPT can guide reflection and personal growth:

“Help me analyze my productivity logs and find where I lose time.”
“Based on my notes, what are the biggest blockers to my goals?”
“Create a habit tracker for my top 3 priorities.”

Make a weekly routine to reflect on:

  • What worked
  • What didn’t
  • What you’ll change next week
