How to structure an AI prompt for optimal results

It's difficult to consistently create content that truly resonates with your target audience. AI tools are extremely useful, but there are still things to consider when creating prompts. After all, you want to get the best results that will enhance your social media marketing efforts.

With that in mind, here are some tips for structuring your prompts to achieve the best results.

Provide context.

As a content marketer, I've lost count of how many articles have told me that 'content is king.'. In the age of artificial intelligence, that phrase may well become 'context is king.'.

Why?

It's simple. When you give an AI tool more information about what you want, it will be able to generate content that is more likely to match your expectations.

Say you want a pepperoni pizza with extra cheese and a thick crust. You wouldn't just call your local pizza parlor and say, "Yo, gimme one of those pizzas."

I mean, you could, but your chances of getting what you want are slim. You'd need to be specific to get the right pizza.

AI prompts function in much the same way. If you provide more context to help the tool understand your goals and preferences, you will be much more likely to receive a satisfactory response.

Keep the following points in mind when writing your prompts, and you will see significantly better results.

1. Be specific.

When you ask an AI tool to assist you with a social media post, make sure to provide some specifics. For example, should it include hashtags or emojis? Do you want it to generate a list? Make it clear in your prompt.

You should also be specific about the length of a post. The request for "a fairly short promotional LinkedIn post" leaves too much room for interpretation of what fairly short means. Instead, you can request "a promotional LinkedIn post that is 2-3 sentences long.".

2. Establish the format.

Each social media platform has specific formats that work well. A funny tweet follows a completely different format than an inspirational LinkedIn post. When using an AI tool, specify both the desired writing style and content.

3. Be as specific and descriptive as possible.

Give the tool as much key information as possible. There is a significant difference between the output you will receive when you provide a basic prompt and one with additional details.

A basic prompt could be something like:

Create a social media post promoting Jake's Cakes.

A prompt that provides more information could be:

"Act as a social media marketing expert and create an engaging Facebook post to promote Jake's Cakes' upcoming community event. The event is called Jake's Big Bake Off and will take place on December 5th. It includes a guest celebrity appearance by Gordon Ramsay. Use the hashtag #JakesBigBakeOff.

However, it is also important to avoid unnecessary information, so do not include extra detail just for the sake of it.

Define the tone of voice.

Each brand has a unique social media presence. It feels different.

You would never confuse a Chipotle tweet with a Visa tweet. Even if I hid the logos and only showed you the messages, you'd know which was which.

That is largely due to the established tone of voice that each brand employs on the platform.

Maintain a consistent tone of voice when creating AI content prompts. Make it clear how you want the response to sound.

Looking for a more inspirational social media message? Great. Tell the AI tool to make it sound positive or to use a hopeful tone of voice. This ensures that the generated content matches your expectations.

Set limitations.

It is reasonable to expect that AI tools understand social media platforms to some extent. If you ask for a series of Twitter posts, all of the results will be within the standard 280-character limit, correct?

No, actually. You will occasionally receive tweet recommendations that exceed 500 characters.

Similarly, when requesting a LinkedIn post, an AI tool may fail to account for the platform's 140-character limit. That's when they see more' button appear.

To ensure that platform-specific details like this are taken into account, include limitations in your prompt.

For example, a prompt for a LinkedIn story could state, "Limit the opening line to 20 words and include a hook that makes me want to read on."

You can also set a limitation by excluding a word or phrase. Sometimes brand guidelines prohibit you from using specific language because it is part of a competitor's core messaging.

In any situation like this, make sure the AI tool understands not to use the term.

Ask for multiple versions.

To ensure success, it's crucial to request multiple variations. Whether you're brainstorming ideas for new social media posts in the early stages of content creation or putting the finishing touches on a draft, seeing multiple versions is always beneficial.

This is also incredibly simple to do. When writing an AI prompt, instead of asking for a single output like "Write a catchy Facebook post," aim for multiple outputs like "Propose five versions of a catchy Facebook post."
