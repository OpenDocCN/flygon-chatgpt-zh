Chapter 1
The Importance of Effective Prompts in
Medical Education

Medical education is a dynamic and demanding field that requires effective teaching methods to ensure that future healthcare professionals are well-prepared to meet the challenges of clinical practice. The use of artificial intelligence (AI), such as ChatGPT, has proven to be a powerful tool in aiding teaching, providing support in various educational activities. However, to maximize the potential of this technology, it is necessary that the prompts – or instructions given to the AI – are clear, detailed, and well-structured.

An effective prompt can transform the teaching work experience, making the task more interactive, practical, and relevant for medical students. These commands must be able to guide the AI to provide accurate and useful responses appropriate to the educational context. In this chapter, we will explore the importance of creating effective prompts in medical education, highlighting the benefits they bring to both faculty and students.

Benefits of Effective Prompts:

  1. Clarity in Communication: Well-crafted prompts help to avoid ambiguity, ensuring that the AI understands exactly what is expected.

  2. Student Engagement: Prompts that encourage critical thinking and the practical application of knowledge increase student engagement.

  3. Facilitation of Teaching: Teachers can use AI to create teaching materials, plan lessons, and develop assessments more efficiently.

  4. Personalization of Learning: AI can tailor responses according to students' level of knowledge, providing a personalized learning experience.

  5. Constant Update: Technology allows the incorporation of recent advances and modern practices, keeping the content always up to date.

Practical examples:

  • Lesson Planning: Create a prompt for the AI to develop a lesson plan on a specific topic, such as the pharmacology of antibiotics.

  • Assessment Development: Use prompts to generate multiple-choice questions or clinical cases that assess students' knowledge effectively.

  • Classroom Discussions: Prompts that promote discussions based on real clinical scenarios, encouraging students to apply theoretical knowledge in practice.

Conclusion

Crafting effective prompts is an essential skill for maximizing the use of ChatGPT in medical education. By investing time in creating clear and detailed instructions, educators can transform their students' learning experience, making it more enriching and efficient for future doctors.

Chapter 2
Identifying the Objective

Creating an effective prompt starts with clearly identifying the goal. Knowing exactly what you hope to achieve with the prompt is key to guiding artificial intelligence in an accurate and useful way. In medical education, the goals can vary widely, from the preparation of educational materials to the elaboration of complex assessments.

Defining the Purpose of the Prompt:

  1. Preparation of Educational Materials: Create didactic content, such as class notes, summaries or presentations.

    • Example: "Develop a summary of the basic principles of pharmacology."

  2. Lesson Planning: Structuring the content of a lesson, defining topics and methodologies.

    • Example: "Develop a lesson plan on the pharmacology of antibiotics."

  3. Presentation Writing: Generate slides or other visual materials for use in the classroom.

    • Example: "Create a slide show about the mechanisms of action of antibiotics."

  4. Creation of Assessments: Develop questions and clinical cases to assess students' knowledge.

    • Example: "Formulate multiple-choice questions about the side effects of antibiotics."

Importance of Objective Identification:

  • Direction and Focus: Clearly defines the scope of work and keeps content relevant and targeted.

  • Efficiency: Avoids wasting time on unnecessary or out-of-context information.

  • Quality: Improves the quality of AI responses, making them more accurate and useful.

Strategies for Setting Clear Goals:

  • Specificity: Be as specific as possible about what you want to achieve.

  • Relevance: Make sure the goal aligns with the educational needs of the students.

  • Realism: Set goals that are achievable within the context and time available.

Practical Example of Goal Identification

"You're a university professor of pharmacology preparing a class for second-year medical students. Create a lesson plan on the pharmacology of antibiotics, covering the following topics: definition of antibiotics, mechanisms of action, main categories, side effects, and clinical cases."

Chapter 3
Contextualization in Medical Education

For a prompt to be effective, it needs to be clearly contextualized, providing artificial intelligence with an adequate understanding of the scenario and the topic to be addressed. In medical education, contextualization helps situate students and AI in the learning environment, making content more relevant and applicable.

Providing Clear Context:

  1. Scenario Presentation: Describe the learning environment and the specific situation.

    • Example: "You are a university professor of pharmacology preparing a class for second-year medical students."

  2. Introduction to the Topic: Introduce the topic that will be discussed, providing an overview.

    • Example: "Today's class is about the pharmacology of antibiotics, an important topic for treating infections."

  3. Content Relevance: Explain why the topic is important and how it applies to the course.

    • Example: "Understanding the pharmacology of antibiotics is essential to prescribing effective treatments and avoiding bacterial resistance."

Importance of Contextualization:

  • Engagement: It helps to capture the attention of students, showing the practical relevance of the content.

  • Clarity: Provides a frame of reference for AI, resulting in more accurate and contextualized responses.

  • Applicability: Facilitates the application of theoretical knowledge in practical and clinical scenarios.

Strategies to Contextualize Effectively:

  • Use of Real Examples: Use scenarios and clinical cases that students may encounter in medical practice.

  • Clarity in Communication: Avoid vague terms and provide specific details about the setting and theme.

  • Relevance: Choose contexts that are pertinent to the students' level of knowledge and the objectives of the course.

Practical Example of Contextualization

"You're a university professor of pharmacology preparing a class for second-year medical students. Today's class is about the pharmacology of antibiotics, an important topic for the treatment of infections. Understanding the pharmacology of antibiotics is essential for prescribing effective treatments and avoiding bacterial resistance. Write a bullet-structured summary for a 50-minute lesson."

Chapter 4
Structuring Content in a Logical Way

One of the keys to creating an effective prompt is structuring the content in a logical and organized way. This ensures that artificial intelligence can follow a clear line of reasoning, addressing all important topics in a cohesive and understandable way.

Dividing Content into Logical Parts:

  1. Introduction: Introduce the topic and its importance.

    • Example: "The pharmacology of antibiotics is critical to the effective treatment of bacterial infections."

  2. Main Topics: List and describe the main points to be covered.

    • Example: "Describe the mechanisms of action of antibiotics, main categories, common side effects, and clinical cases."

  3. Detailed Subtopics: Provide details and practical examples for each main topic.

    • Example: "Within the main categories, include penicillins, cephalosporins, tetracyclines, and macrolides, with their clinical applications and side effects."

  4. Conclusion: Summarize the main points and highlight the practical application of knowledge.

    • Example: "Conclude by discussing the importance of the correct choice of antibiotic to avoid bacterial resistance."

Importance of Logical Structuring:

  • Ease of Understanding: Helps both AI and students understand and follow the content clearly.

  • Cohesion: Ensures that all parts of the content are connected in a logical and fluid way.

  • Comprehensiveness: Ensures that all important topics are covered in detail.

Strategies for Structuring Content:

  • Use of Headings and Subheadings: Organize content into clear and distinct sections.

  • Logical Sequence: Follow a natural progression of topics, from the most general to the most specific.

  • Lists and Bullets: Use lists and bullets to highlight important points and make them easier to read.

Practical Example of Structuring:

  1. Introduction:

    • Definition of antibiotics.

    • Importance in the treatment of infections.

  2. Mechanisms of Action:

    • How antibiotics fight bacteria.

    • Practical examples of each category.

  3. Main Categories:

    • Penicillins, cephalosporins, tetracyclines, macrolids, among others.

    • Clinical applications and use cases.

  4. Side Effects:

    • Common (e.g., allergic reactions) and rare (e.g., renal toxicity).

  5. Clinical Cases:

    • Examples for discussion in class.

  6. Current Technologies:

    • Use of AI in the development of new antibiotics.

Chapter 5
The Importance of Detail and Specificity

For a prompt to be truly effective, it's essential to incorporate specific details. The more detailed the prompt, the more accurate the artificial intelligence response will be, providing rich and relevant content for students.

Incorporating Specific Details:

  1. Describe the Topics in Depth: Detail each topic that should be covered.

    • Example: "Include practical examples from each category of antibiotics and discuss their clinical applications."

  2. Provide Examples and Case Studies: Utilize concrete examples to illustrate key points.

    • Example: "Describe a clinical case where a patient has resistance to a particular antibiotic and how this was managed."

  3. Specify Parameters and Limitations: Define the parameters within which the AI should operate.

    • Example: "Discuss only antibiotics used to treat common bacterial infections, excluding experimental treatments."

Importance of Detail and Specificity:

  • Accuracy: Detailed prompts result in more accurate and relevant responses.

  • Completeness: Ensures that all important aspects of the topic are covered.

  • Engagement: Specific details make the content more interesting and applicable, increasing student engagement.

Strategies for Incorporating Details:

  • Research and Preparation: Do some research beforehand to ensure that all relevant details are included in the prompt.

  • Specific Questions: Formulate specific questions that target the AI to provide detailed answers.

  • Review and Refinement: Review the prompt several times to add more details and eliminate ambiguity.

Practical Example of Detailing

"You're a university professor of pharmacology preparing a class for second-year medical students. Create a lesson plan on the pharmacology of antibiotics, covering the following topics: definition of antibiotics, mechanisms of action, main categories, common side effects, and clinical cases. Include practical examples from each category of antibiotics and discuss their clinical applications. Discuss how new technologies, such as artificial intelligence, are being used to develop new antibiotics. Write a bullet-structured summary for a 50-minute lesson."

Stimulating Critical Thinking in Medical Education

An essential component in creating effective prompts is the ability to stimulate critical thinking among students. In medical education, this means encouraging students to analyze, evaluate, and apply theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios.

Encouraging Reflection and Practical Application:

  1. Formulate Analytical Questions: Include questions that require critical analysis and deep reflection.

    • Example: "How would you apply antibiotic knowledge in a clinical setting involving a patient with a resistant hospital-acquired infection?"

  2. Develop Clinical Scenarios: Use clinical cases that challenge students to apply the knowledge acquired.

    • Example: "Describe a case in which a patient does not respond to treatment with common antibiotics. What would be the next steps in the management of this patient?"

  3. Promote Group Discussions: Encourage collaboration and the exchange of ideas among students.

    • Example: "Discuss as a group the possible causes of bacterial resistance in hospitalized patients and propose strategies to overcome this problem."

Importance of Critical Thinking:

  • Problem Solving: Helps students develop problem-solving skills, which are essential in medical practice.

  • Decision Making: Prepares students to make informed, evidence-based decisions in clinical situations.

  • Engagement: Promotes more active and engaged learning, keeping students motivated and interested.

Strategies to Stimulate Critical Thinking:

  • Realistic Scenarios: Use real and challenging clinical cases that reflect situations encountered in medical practice.

  • Open-ended Questions: Formulate open-ended questions that encourage reflection and exploration of multiple perspectives.

  • Continuous Feedback: Provide constructive and ongoing feedback to help students improve their critical thinking skills.

Practical Example of Stimulating Critical Thinking

"You're a university professor of pharmacology preparing a class for second-year medical students. Create a lesson plan on the pharmacology of antibiotics, including a clinical case where a patient has resistance to common antibiotics. Include questions to the students how they would deal with this case and what strategies they would use to overcome bacterial resistance."

Clear and Logical Structure and Formatting

For a prompt to be effective, it must be presented in a clear and organized manner. Structure and formatting play a crucial role in the way artificial intelligence interprets and responds to instructions, as well as making it easier for students to understand.

Arranging the Prompt in a Clear and Logical Way:

  1. Use of Headings and Subheadings: Divide content into distinct sections to improve readability.

    • Example: "1. Introduction, 2. Mechanisms of Action, 3. Main Categories, 4. Side Effects, 5. Clinical Cases."

  2. Lists and Bullets: Use lists and bullets to highlight important points and steps.

    • Example:

      • "Penicillins: Used in the treatment of bacterial infections. Examples: Amoxicillin, Penicillin V."

      • "Cephalosporins: Effective against a wide range of bacteria. Examples: Cephalexin, Ceftriaxone."

  3. Logical Sequence: Present the topics in an order that makes sense, facilitating the progression of learning.

    • Example: "Start with the definition of antibiotics, followed by the mechanisms of action, major categories, side effects, and finally clinical cases."

Importance of Structure and Formatting:

  • Clarity: Promotes clear communication and avoids misunderstandings.

  • Ease of Reading: Facilitates the reading and assimilation of the content by students.

  • Efficiency: Allows AI to respond more accurately and organized.

Strategies for Structuring and Formatting It Effectively:

  • Consistency: Maintain a consistent format throughout the entire prompt.

  • Split into Steps: Break content down into smaller, manageable chunks.

  • Proofread: Review the prompt to make sure it is clear and free of ambiguity.

Review and Adjustments: Ensuring Prompt Quality

Proofreading and tweaking are necessary steps in creating an effective prompt. Ensuring that the prompt is clear, complete, and free of ambiguity is essential for getting accurate and useful responses from artificial intelligence. You can utilize ChatGPT itself to revise your prompt.

Reviewing and Refining the Prompt:

  1. Clarity Check: Make sure the prompt is clear and understandable.

    • Example: "Review the prompt to ensure that all instructions are explicit and easy to understand."

  2. Completeness: Ensure that the prompt covers all the necessary topics.

    • Example: "Ensure that all important aspects of antibiotic pharmacology are included."

  3. Eliminate Ambiguities: Identify and correct any ambiguous language.

    • Example: "Review the prompt to remove vague or confusing terms that could lead to inaccurate answers."

  4. Feedback: Solicit feedback from peers or experts to improve the prompt.

    • Example: "Ask other core class teachers to review the prompt and suggest improvements."

Importance of Review and Adjustments:

  • Accuracy: Improves the accuracy of AI responses, resulting in high-quality content.

  • Efficiency: Reduces the time required for later corrections and adjustments.

  • Quality: Ensures that the prompt is of high quality, providing a better learning experience for students.

Strategies for Reviewing and Adjusting the Prompt:

  • Close Reading: Review the prompt several times to identify any potential improvements.

  • Practice Test: Utilize the prompt in a real-world scenario to see how the AI responds and make adjustments as needed.

  • Consultation with Experts: Work with colleagues or experts to gain different perspectives and suggestions for improvement.

  • Use ChatGPT: Ask the tool itself to review your prompt and suggest improvements to it.

Chapter 12
Clinical Cases: Applying Prompts in Practice

The use of clinical cases is an effective strategy to apply theoretical knowledge in practice, especially in medical education. Well-crafted prompts can help create realistic clinical cases that challenge students to think critically and apply their knowledge.

Developing Clinical Cases:

  1. Patient History: Provide detailed information about the patient, including age, gender, medical history, and current symptoms.

    • Example: "A 45-year-old male patient with a history of type 2 diabetes with a high fever, productive cough, and chest pain."

  2. Test Results: Include relevant laboratory and imaging test results.

    • Example: "Chest X-ray showing bilateral pulmonary infiltrates. Blood cultures positive for methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA)."

  3. Diagnosis and Discussion: Ask students to formulate a diagnosis and discuss possible causes and complications.

    • Example: "Based on the test results, what is the most likely diagnosis? What are the possible complications?"

  4. Treatment Plan: Encourage students to develop a detailed treatment plan.

    • Example: "Describe the initial treatment plan for this patient, including the choice of antibiotics and rationale."

  5. Follow-up and Evaluation: Include information about patient follow-up and assessment of response to treatment.

    • Example: "The patient returns after 48 hours of treatment with vancomycin. Assess response to treatment and adjust plan as needed."

Importance of Clinical Cases:

  • Practical Application: Allows students to apply theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios, developing essential clinical skills.

  • Critical Thinking: Challenges students to think critically and make informed decisions based on clinical data.

  • Preparation for Practice: Prepares students for real-world situations they will encounter in medical practice.

Strategies for Creating Effective Clinical Cases:

  • Clinical Relevance: Choose cases that are representative of common and relevant clinical situations.

  • Detail: Provide enough detail to allow for deep analysis, but avoid excessive information that could confuse students.

  • Feedback: Provide constructive feedback after the case discussion to help students learn from the experience.

Practical Example of a Clinical Case

"You're a university professor of pharmacology preparing a clinical case for second-year medical students. Develop a case in which a 60-year-old female patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presents with acute respiratory infection. Include patient history, laboratory and imaging test results, and ask students to formulate a diagnosis and develop a treatment plan. Discuss the possible complications and the importance of patient follow-up."

Chapter 13
Challenges and Solutions in Prompt Creation

Creating effective prompts in medical education can present several challenges. Identifying these challenges and developing practical solutions is essential to ensure that prompts are clear, accurate, and useful.

Common Challenges in Creating Prompts:

  1. Ambiguity: Vague or ambiguous prompts can lead to inaccurate responses from the AI.

    • Solution: Review the prompt to eliminate vague terms and provide clear and specific instructions.

  2. Too Much Information: Providing too much information can overwhelm the AI and students.

    • Solution: Break down content into smaller, manageable chunks, focusing on the main points.

  3. Lack of Context: The absence of clear context can make it difficult to understand and apply the content.

    • Solution: Include an introduction that situates AI and students in the right educational setting.

  4. Relevance: Irrelevant or outdated prompts can demotivate students.

    • Solution: Update prompts regularly to reflect the latest practices and technologies.

  5. Complexity: Overly complex prompts can be difficult to understand and follow.

    • Solution: Simplify the language and structure of prompts while keeping the focus on clarity and objectivity.

Practical Solutions to Improve Prompts:

  • Continuous Feedback: Solicit feedback from colleagues and students to identify areas for improvement.

  • Pilot Tests: Utilize pilot tests to evaluate the effectiveness of prompts before using them in real lessons.

  • Proofreading and Editing: Proofread and edit prompts multiple times to ensure clarity and accuracy.

  • Collaboration: Work with other teachers and experts to develop more robust and effective prompts.

  • Use of Examples: Include clear and specific examples to illustrate the prompt's instructions.

Importance of Addressing Challenges in Creating Prompts:

  • Quality of Teaching: Effective prompts improve the quality of teaching and student learning.

  • Engagement: Clear and relevant prompts increase student engagement and motivation.

  • AI performance: Well-crafted prompts ensure that the AI provides accurate and useful answers, making teachers' jobs easier.

Practical Example of Approaching Challenges

Create a lesson plan on the pharmacology of antibiotics, covering the following topics: definition of antibiotics, mechanisms of action, main categories, common side effects, and clinical cases.
